Beast / False Prophet / Mark

Authority Structures and Worship-Loyalty in Prophetic Symbol

Framework stub: this locks non-speculative categories so chapters don’t invent definitions.

1) What “beast” language usually means in the prophets

In biblical apocalyptic, beasts portray empires, authorities, and power-systems—especially when they oppose covenant faithfulness. The symbol is about rule, coercion, and allegiance, not a literal monster.

2) The “mark” as identity / allegiance language

“Mark” language functions as belonging and worship-loyalty imagery. Read it as a covenant sign of allegiance, not as a modern microchip theory.

3) What this is NOT

  • Not a prediction of modern tech implants.
  • Not a future-only end-times schedule.
  • Not a permission slip for fear-based speculation.
