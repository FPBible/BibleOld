Preface to 2 Peter

The Promise of His Presence and the Passing of the Old Order

Purpose of the Letter

Written shortly before his death, Peter's second letter is a final warning to the transition generation. He addresses "scoffers" who were beginning to doubt the timing of the parousia (presence) of Christ because the physical world appeared to be continuing as normal.

The “Elements” (Stoicheia)

Traditional futurism claims Chapter 3 describes the literal destruction of the planet. However, a fulfilled perspective recognizes that the “elements” (stoicheia) refer to the elementary components of the Old Covenant system—the temple, the sacrifices, and the legal shadows.

  • Covenant Heavens and Earth: Not the physical globe, but the covenantal order established at Sinai.
  • Fervent Heat: The consuming fire of judgment that fell upon Jerusalem in AD 70.
  • New Heavens and New Earth: The fully established New Covenant kingdom where righteousness dwells.

Peter’s message is clear: The “Day of the Lord” was not thousands of years away; it was the imminent closure of the Jewish age that would vindicate the saints and permanently remove the Old Order.
