1 Corinthians — Appendix

Key Themes, Terms, and the Resurrection Framework

This appendix gives orientation for reading 1 Corinthians consistently. It is not a running commentary. It highlights the recurring contrasts and the covenant logic behind Paul’s corrections.

1) “Carnal” vs “Spiritual”

In 1 Corinthians, “carnal” is not “having a body.” It is living from old identity systems: rivalry, status, ego, faction. “Spiritual” is living from the Spirit: love, unity, edification, maturity.

2) Temple Language

Paul calls believers God’s temple. This is covenant replacement language. The temple is no longer a building-centred system defining access. God’s presence is now embodied in a people.

3) The Lord’s Table

The Lord’s Supper is treated as a covenant meal of unity. Divisions at the table are not “bad manners.” They are a denial of the New Covenant reality: one body, one bread, one shared life.

4) Gifts and Order

Gifts are not badges. They are tools for building up. Paul’s goal is not suppression of the Spirit, but the protection of love and the strengthening of the body.

5) Resurrection (1 Corinthians 15)

Paul anchors resurrection in Christ’s victory. He contrasts:

  • perishable vs imperishable
  • dishonour vs glory
  • weakness vs power
  • natural body vs spiritual body

In this project’s fulfilled lens, “resurrection” is read through the Bible’s own patterns: Ezekiel 37 (restoration), Isaiah (new creation language), and covenant transition realities. The point is not an escape story. The point is victory and transformation in the new age.
