Luke 4

The Last Adam • The Year of Release • The Synagogue Departure

1. Immediate Context

Luke 4 documents the formal legal confrontation between the New Creation and the powers of the Old World. Following His baptism, Jesus is led into the wilderness to face the testing where the first Adam failed and where national Israel (the corporate son) wandered for 40 years. This 40-day wilderness ordeal is the legal prerequisite for the 40-year transition (AD 30–70). By quoting Deuteronomy 8:3, Jesus establishes Himself as the "Last Adam," the man of the Spirit who is legally qualified to terminate the era of the first man.

The scene then shifts to the synagogue in Nazareth, the local epicenter of religious performance. Jesus makes a formal proclamation of Jubilee—the legal cancellation of all debt under the Mosaic system. The violent reaction of the religious establishment serves as a microcosm of the AD 70 horizon: the "Thief" (institutional mediation) cannot coexist with the reality of direct access. Jesus’s sovereign departure through the middle of the murderous crowd signifies that the Kingdom of the Spirit is not subject to the physical jurisdiction or the violence of the Old Covenant system.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/LUK04.htm

1 Jesus, full of the Holy Spirit, returned from the Jordan, and was led by the Spirit into the wilderness 2 for forty days, being tempted by the devil. He ate nothing in those days. Afterward, when they were completed, he was hungry. 3 The devil said to him, “If you are the Son of God, command this stone to become bread.”
4 Jesus answered him, saying, “It is written, ‘Every man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word of God.’”
5 The devil, leading him up on a high mountain, showed him all the kingdoms of the world in a moment of time. 6 The devil said to him, “I will give you all this authority, and their glory, for it has been delivered to me; and I give it to whomever I want. 7 If you therefore will worship before me, it will all be yours.”
8 Jesus answered him, “Get behind me, Satan! For it is written, ‘You shall worship the Lord your God, and him only shall you serve.’”
9 He led him to Jerusalem, and set him on the pinnacle of the temple, and said to him, “If you are the Son of God, cast yourself down from here, 10 for it is written, ‘He will give his angels charge concerning you, to guard you;’ 11 and, ‘On their hands they will bear you up, lest perhaps you dash your foot against a stone.’”
12 Jesus answering, said to him, “It has been said, ‘You shall not tempt the Lord your God.’”
13 When the devil had completed every temptation, he departed from him until another time.
14 Jesus returned in the power of the Spirit into Galilee, and news about him spread through all the surrounding area. 15 He taught in their synagogues, being glorified by all. 16 He came to Nazareth, where he had been brought up. He entered, as was his custom, into the synagogue on the Sabbath day, and stood up to read. 17 The book of the prophet Isaiah was handed to him. He opened the book, and found the place where it was written,
18 “The Spirit of the Lord is on me, because he has anointed me to preach good news to the poor. He has sent me to heal the brokenhearted, to proclaim release to the captives, recovering of sight to the blind, to deliver those who are crushed, 19 and to proclaim the acceptable year of the Lord.”
20 He closed the book, gave it back to the attendant, and sat down. The eyes of all in the synagogue were fastened on him. 21 He began to tell them, “Today, this Scripture has been fulfilled in your hearing.”
22 All testified about him, and wondered at the gracious words which proceeded out of his mouth. They said, “Isn’t this Joseph’s son?”
23 He said to them, “Doubtless you will tell me this proverb, ‘Physician, heal yourself! Whatever we have heard done at Capernaum, do also here in your hometown.’” 24 He said, “Most certainly I tell you, no prophet is acceptable in his hometown. 25 But I tell you in truth, there were many widows in Israel in the days of Elijah, when the sky was shut up three years and six months, when a great famine came over all the land. 26 Elijah was sent to none of them, except to Zarephath, in the land of Sidon, to a woman who was a widow. 27 There were many lepers in Israel in the time of Elisha the prophet, yet not one of them was cleansed, except Naaman the Syrian.”
28 They were all filled with wrath in the synagogue, as they heard these things. 29 They rose up, threw him out of the city, and led him to the brow of the hill that their city was built on, that they might throw him off the cliff. 30 But he, passing through the middle of them, went his way.
31 He came down to Capernaum, a city of Galilee. He was teaching them on the Sabbath day. 32 They were astonished at his teaching, for his word was with authority. 33 In the synagogue there was a man who had a spirit of an unclean demon; and he cried out with a loud voice, 34 saying, “Ah! what have we to do with you, Jesus of Nazareth? Have you come to destroy us? I know you who you are: the Holy One of God!”
35 Jesus rebuked him, saying, “Be silent, and come out of him!” When the demon had thrown him down in the middle, he came out of him, having done him no harm. 36 Amazement came on all, and they spoke together, one with another, saying, “What is this word? For with authority and power he commands the unclean spirits, and they come out!” 37 News about him went out into every place of the surrounding region.
38 He rose up from the synagogue, and entered into Simon’s house. Simon’s mother-in-law was suffering from a great fever, and they asked him about her. 39 He stood over her, and rebuked the fever; and it left her. Immediately she rose up and served them. 40 When the sun was setting, all those who had any sick with various diseases brought them to him; and he laid his hands on every one of them, and healed them. 41 Demons also came out from many, crying out, and saying, “You are the Christ, the Son of God!” Rebuking them, he didn’t allow them to speak, because they knew that he was the Christ.
42 When it was day, he departed and went into a deserted place, and the multitudes looked for him, and came to him, and held him, so that he would not go from them. 43 But he said to them, “I must preach the good news of the Kingdom of God to the other cities also. For this reason I have been sent.” 44 He was preaching in the synagogues of Galilee.

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • Forty Days (v. 2): Parallels the 40-year generation (Hebrews 3:9) allowed for the legal transition. Jesus succeeds where the corporate "son" (Israel) failed, proving Himself the qualified Heir of the New World.
  • Kingdoms of the World (v. 5): Greek oikoumene. The Roman-centric age. The "authority" claimed by the adversary was the legal dominion over the Old Covenant world, which Jesus systematically reclaimed through His obedience to the Word.
  • Release (v. 18): Greek aphesis. Legally denotes a pardon, a dismissal of charges, or a cancellation of debt. Jesus declares the "debt of the Law" (Colossians 2:14) to be finished.
  • Today (v. 21): Sēmeron. Audience relevance is absolute. The fulfillment was not postponed; it was present in the first century, marking the formal "activation" of the transition.

4. Annotations

  • Pinnacle of the Temple (v. 9) — The headquarters of the religious establishment. The adversary attempts to use the "Thief's" center to trap the King into a performance-based validation of His status.
  • Acceptable Year of the Lord (v. 19) — The Jubilee. Under the Old Covenant (Leviticus 25), this meant the return of inheritance and freedom for slaves. Jesus applies this legally to the New Creation as a permanent reality.
  • Widows and Lepers (vv. 25-27) — Elijah and Elisha bypassing Israel to heal Gentiles. This was a legal indictment against the synagogue's claim of exclusive mediation, predicting the global Royal Priesthood.
  • Have you come to destroy us? (v. 34) — The "unclean spirits" of the old age recognize that the King’s arrival is the literal destruction of their legal jurisdiction over humanity.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

1) The Legal Triumph of the Last Adam

Jesus’s wilderness ordeal was the legal undoing of the first Adam’s failure. In the wilderness, Jesus is tested as the representative of the new humanity. Where the first Adam failed over food, and Israel failed over testing God in their 40-year wandering, Jesus uses "It is written" (Luke 4:4) as the legal shield. He proves that life in the New Creation is defined by the Word of God, not by the performance of physical rituals or the acquisition of earthly power. He is the first man of the New Creation to stand in perfect legal standing, qualifying Him to legally "shut down" the old Adamic era. This victory ensures that the New Creation is built on the foundation of a finished work of obedience, rather than the Exhausting Performance of the Law. The adversary’s attempt to offer the "glory" of the old world was a legal maneuver to keep the King bound to the old age; Jesus’s refusal was the legal declaration of the new age’s arrival.

2) The Jubilee Decree and the End of Spiritual Debt

When Jesus reads from Isaiah 61, He is issuing a formal proclamation of Release (Jubilee). The Old Covenant system had become a "ministry of condemnation" and debt, where the people were perpetual debtors to a Law they could not keep. The Jubilee Jesus announces is the legal cancellation of that debt for all who enter the New Creation. This "Release" (Luke 4:18) is the legal foundation of our present rest; we are no longer "captives" to the performance-based mediation of the Temple or its modern denominational equivalents. By declaring that this Scripture was fulfilled "Today" (Luke 4:21), Jesus signals that the transition generation had officially entered the year of amnesty. The "Acceptable Year" is not a recurring event but a permanent covenantal status for the Royal Priesthood. We do not work for release; we live from a state of established release.

3) The Rejection at Nazareth and the AD 70 Horizon

The synagogue’s attempt to murder Jesus (Luke 4:29) is the instinctive reaction of the "Thief." When the religious establishment hears that God's grace is not restricted to their biological lineage or institutional mediation—demonstrated by the examples of Elijah and Elisha—their only response is violence. This rejection at the start of His ministry foreshadows the national rejection that led to the "fire" of AD 70. Jesus’s sovereign departure through the crowd signifies that the New Creation is not subject to the power of religious mobs or dead institutions. He "passed through the middle of them," demonstrating that His life and Kingdom are governed by a higher legal authority. The Synagogue of Nazareth represents the Old World attempting to kill its own fulfillment; the King’s departure represents the legal "leaving desolate" of the system that refuses the transition.

4) Jurisdiction Over the Curse of the Old World

The healings and exorcisms in Capernaum (Luke 4:35-41) are legal demonstrations of the King's jurisdiction over the "Land" and its inhabitants. He rebukes "fevers" and "demons" with the same authoritative word used in the wilderness. He is systematically clearing the old world's curse to make room for the established reality of the Kingdom. The demons’ cry, "Have you come to destroy us?" (Luke 4:34), is a recognition that the arrival of the New Creation is the literal destruction of the old world's spiritual hierarchy. Jesus rebukes their testimony because He does not require validation from the "powers" of the age that is passing away. These acts are not mere wonders; they are legal evictions of the old age’s occupants, proving that the King has "bound the strong man" and is now plundering his house during the transition generation.

8. New Creation Reality

In Luke 4, the "Today" of fulfillment has dawned (Luke 4:21). You are no longer living in the "Temptation Wilderness"—you are living in the "Proclaimed Release." The debt of the Law has been legally cancelled; the Year of Favor is your permanent covenantal environment. You do not need to perform to be "acceptable," because the King has already proclaimed you so. You do not belong to the Synagogue of performance; you belong to the Kingdom of Rest. The "Thief" (religious mediation) has no legal claim over you, and his authority was stripped in the first century. You possess direct access to the Father, free from the tithing, rituals, and mediation of professional priests. You are the freed captive, walking in the light of the Rising Sun.
