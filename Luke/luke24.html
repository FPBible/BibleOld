Luke 24

The Living Sovereign • The Opened Scriptures • The Ascension to Glory

1. Immediate Context

Luke 24 documents the formal inauguration of the New Creation. During the transition (AD 30–70), the resurrection of the Son was the definitive legal evidence that the Adamic curse had been broken and the Old Covenant "Week" was forever finished. The empty tomb is not just a miracle of biology; it is a jurisdictional fact proving that the Prince of Life has exhausted the grave's power and now holds the keys to the New World.

The chapter unfolds as a sequence of "openings": the tomb is opened, the eyes of the disciples are opened, and the Scriptures are opened. The Son's walk to Emmaus serves as the final hermeneutical key for the Royal Priesthood, teaching them that all of Moses and the Prophets were shadows pointing to this exultant victory. As the AD 70 horizon approached, the Son’s ascension to the Father's Right Hand marked the beginning of His active jurisdictional reign from the heavens. The transition was legally complete; the King had won, the Spirit was promised, and the New Creation was now the established reality for all who believed.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/LUK24.htm

1 But on the first day of the week, at early dawn, they and some others with them came to the tomb, bringing the spices which they had prepared. 2 They found the stone rolled away from the tomb. 3 They entered in, and didn’t find the Lord Jesus’ body. 4 It happened, while they were greatly perplexed about this, behold, two men stood by them in dazzling clothing. 5 Becoming terrified and bowing their faces down to the earth, they said to them, “Why do you seek the living among the dead? 6 He isn’t here, but is risen. Remember what he told you when he was still in Galilee, 7 saying that the Son of Man must be delivered up into the hands of sinful men, and be crucified, and the third day rise again?”
8 They remembered his words, 9 returned from the tomb, and told all these things to the eleven, and to all the rest. 10 Now they were Mary Magdalene, Joanna, and Mary the mother of James. The other women with them told these things to the apostles. 11 These words seemed to them to be quite idle talk, and they didn’t believe them. 12 But Peter got up and ran to the tomb. Stooping and looking in, he saw the linen cloths lying by themselves, and he departed to his home, wondering what had happened.
13 Behold, two of them were going that very day to a village named Emmaus, which was sixty stadia from Jerusalem. 14 They talked with each other about all of these things which had happened. 15 It happened, while they talked and questioned together, that Jesus himself came near, and went with them. 16 But their eyes were kept from recognizing him. 17 He said to them, “What are these words that you are exchanging with each other as you are walking and are sad?”
18 One of them, named Cleopas, answered him, “Are you the only stranger in Jerusalem who doesn’t know the things which have happened there in these days?”
19 He said to them, “What things?” They said to him, “The things concerning Jesus, the Nazarene, who was a prophet mighty in deed and word before God and all the people; 20 and how the chief priests and our rulers delivered him up to be condemned to death, and crucified him. 21 But we were hoping that it was he who would redeem Israel. Yes, and besides all this, it is now the third day since these things happened. 22 Also, certain women of our company amazed us, having been early at the tomb; 23 and when they didn’t find his body, they came, saying that they had also seen a vision of angels, who said that he was alive. 24 Some of us went to the tomb, and found it just like the women had said, but they didn’t see him.”
25 He said to them, “Foolish men, and slow of heart to believe in all that the prophets have spoken! 26 Wasn’t it necessary for the Christ to suffer these things, and to enter into his glory?” 27 Beginning from Moses and from all the prophets, he explained to them in all the Scriptures the things concerning himself.
28 They drew near to the village where they were going, and he acted as though he would go further. 29 They urged him, saying, “Stay with us, for it is almost evening, and the day is almost over.” He went in to stay with them. 30 It happened, that when he had sat down at the table with them, he took the bread and blessed it. Breaking it, he gave it to them. 31 Their eyes were opened, and they recognized him, and he vanished out of their sight. 32 They said to one another, “Wasn’t our heart burning within us, while he spoke to us on the way, and while he opened the Scriptures to us?”
33 They rose up that very hour, and returned to Jerusalem, and found the eleven gathered together, and those who were with them, 34 saying, “The Lord is risen indeed, and has appeared to Simon!” 35 They related the things that happened on the way, and how he was recognized by them in the breaking of the bread.
36 As they said these things, Jesus himself stood in the middle of them, and said to them, “Peace be to you.”
37 But they were terrified and filled with fear, and supposed that they had seen a spirit. 38 He said to them, “Why are you troubled? Why do doubts arise in your hearts? 39 See my hands and my feet, that it is I myself. Touch me and see, for a spirit doesn’t have flesh and bones, as you see that I have.” 40 When he had said this, he showed them his hands and his feet. 41 While they still didn’t believe for joy, and wondered, he said to them, “Do you have anything here to eat?” 42 They gave him a piece of a broiled fish and some honeycomb. 43 He took them, and ate in their presence.
44 He said to them, “This is what I told you, while I was still with you, that all things which are written in the law of Moses, the prophets, and the psalms concerning me must be fulfilled.” 45 Then he opened their minds, that they might understand the Scriptures. 46 He said to them, “Thus it is written, and thus it was necessary for the Christ to suffer and to rise from the dead the third day, 47 and that repentance and remission of sins should be preached in his name to all the nations, beginning at Jerusalem. 48 You are witnesses of these things. 49 Behold, I send forth the promise of my Father on you. But wait in the city of Jerusalem until you are clothed with power from on high.”
50 He led them out as far as to Bethany, and he lifted up his hands, and blessed them. 51 It happened, while he blessed them, that he withdrew from them, and was carried up into heaven. 52 They worshiped him, and returned to Jerusalem with great joy, 53 and were continually in the temple, praising and blessing God. Amen.

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • First Day of the Week (v. 1): The legal beginning of the New Creation. The Old Week of Adamic toil is finished; the "Eighth Day" has dawned as the permanent day of rest and resurrection.
  • Must be Fulfilled (v. 44): The Son declares the total legal exhaustion of the Old Covenant shadows. Law, Prophets, and Psalms have reached their terminus in His finished victory.
  • Beginning at Jerusalem (v. 47): The center of the transition. The New World mandate begins at the legal heart of the old system, announcing its foreclosure and the opening of grace to all nations.
  • Carried up into Heaven (v. 51): The Ascension. This is the formal coronation of the King, where He takes His seat at the Right Hand to oversee the AD 30–70 transition and the final removal of the old house.

4. Annotations

  • Two Men (v. 4) — Likely representing the "Two Witnesses" of the transition. Their dazzling clothing signifies the manifest light of the New Creation invading the old world's darkness.
  • Emmaus (v. 13) — Meaning "Warm Springs." A place of restoration. The walk to Emmaus symbolizes the journey of the remnant from the "sadness" of the old world to the "joy" of the New.
  • Breaking of Bread (v. 30, 35) — The jurisdictional sign of the New Covenant. The Sovereign is recognized not through Temple rituals, but through the intimacy of His provided feast.
  • Honeycomb (v. 42) — Symbol of the sweetness of the Land of Promise. The Son eating it proves that the New Creation is a tangible, "flesh and bones" reality, not a ghostly philosophy.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

1) The Manifestation of the Living Sovereign

The empty tomb (Luke 24:1-12) unveils the Son’s glory as the Conqueror of the Adamic curse. The angels’ question—"Why do you seek the living among the dead?"—is the legal verdict of the New Creation. Death no longer has jurisdictional authority over the Sovereign. By rising on the "first day," the Messiah inaugurated an entirely new week for humanity, where the "Performance" of the old Sabbath is replaced by the "Life" of the Resurrection. This event signaled the absolute success of the transition: the legal debt was paid, the grave was plundered, and the King was alive. The "perplexity" of the disciples was the shock of a world transitioning from the shadows of a tomb to the dazzling light of an established victory.

2) The Opened Scriptures on the Road to Restoration

The encounter on the road to Emmaus (Luke 24:13-32) is the exultant unveiling of the Son’s role as the Great Interpreter. Cleopas and his companion were trapped in the sadness of the old world’s perspective, hoping for a "redemption" that fit their nationalistic expectations. The Son corrected their heart-slowness by opening the Scriptures—revealing that the entire Old Covenant was a testimony of His suffering and glory. This walk was the legal briefing for the New Creation: the Law was not a cage, but a road map to the King. When their eyes were opened in the "breaking of the bread," they recognized that the Sovereign was not a "stranger" in the land, but the Host of the New World. This commentary affirms that we live in the light of this "opened" word, where every shadow of the past has been burned away by the heat of His presence.

3) The Remission of Sins and the Global Mandate

The Son’s appearance to the eleven (Luke 24:36-49) establishes the jurisdictional platform of the Royal Priesthood. By showing His "hands and feet," He proved the physical reality of His victory—He is the "Living One" who has survived the judgment. He then delivered the legal constitution of the mission: repentance and "remission of sins" were to be preached in His name to all nations. This was the "Year of Release" (Jubilee) becoming a global law. The Son opened their minds to the "Law, Prophets, and Psalms," ensuring they understood that the transition was the fulfillment of all divine intent. The "Power from on high" was the promised fuel for this expansion, signaling that the New Creation would not be built by human might, but by the Spirit of the Victor.

4) The Ascension: The King’s Final Entry to the Throne

The chapter and the Gospel reach their exultant climax with the Ascension (Luke 24:50-53). As the Son "withdrew from them" and was "carried up into heaven," He was taking His seat as the only valid High Priest and King. His lifted hands in blessing were the last thing they saw—a jurisdictional sign of perpetual favor. This event marked the beginning of the "Heavenly Session" that would oversee the final collapse of the physical Temple in AD 70. The disciples’ return to Jerusalem with "great joy" proves that the sadness of the cross had been legally replaced by the rest of the Resurrection. This commentary highlights that we inhabit a world where the King is seated, the victory is finished, and the "Amen" of God has been spoken over all creation. The Temple in Jerusalem was still standing, but the true Temple was now at the Father's Right Hand.

In Luke 24, the glory of the Son is revealed as the power that opens every door and restores every heart. As a citizen of the New Creation, you are the one for whom the "Stone" has been rolled away and the "Scriptures" have been opened. You do not live in the "sadness" of the old world or the "doubt" of a performance-based system; you live in the "Joy" of the Resurrection. The "Thief" of death and religious confusion has been put to flight. You inhabit a world where the King is "seated at the right hand," continually blessing you with His lifted hands. You have been "clothed with power" and granted the status of a witness to a finished victory. The transition is complete; you possess the "remission of sins" as your legal standing, resting in the warmth of the Son's presence forever.
