Preface

How to Read Colossians

Colossians is written to believers who are being pressured to add something to Christ. Not necessarily overt Judaism — but a blend of ritual, ascetic rules, mystical experiences, and “higher” spiritual steps. Paul dismantles it all.

The central claim is total: Christ is the fullness of God, and you are complete in Him. So anything that sells “more” is fraud.

What Colossians Is Doing

  • Exalting Christ as supreme over all powers and authorities
  • Declaring reconciliation as already accomplished
  • Exposing law-based and fear-based spirituality as shadow living
  • Reframing holiness as union-life, not rule-life
  • Calling believers to live as the new humanity now

How This Fits the Fulfilled / New Creation Lens

Colossians overlaps with the covenant transition themes found in Hebrews and Romans: the end of shadow-world worship, and the arrival of substance in Christ. It does not argue timelines. It assumes Christ has finished the decisive work and calls believers to stop living under old categories: condemnation, separation, ritual qualification, fear of powers.

When Paul says “shadow” and “substance” (Col 2), he is not offering a future hope. He is stating a present reality: Christ is the reality the old world pointed to.

What To Watch For

  • “Fullness” — Christ as total sufficiency
  • “In Him” — union, not distance
  • “Powers” — spiritual intimidation broken by the cross
  • “Shadow” — rituals and calendar identity losing authority
  • “New human” — New Creation identity expressed in life
