The Two Witnesses: The Spirit’s Testimony

From Joshua and Zerubbabel...

In Zechariah 4, the "two anointed ones" (Joshua the Priest and Zerubbabel the King) provide the continuous flow of oil to the lampstand. They represent the two vital offices of the covenant community. However, their identity reaches its prophetic climax in Revelation 11:3-4, where John explicitly identifies his "Two Witnesses" as the "two olive trees and the two lampstands."

...To the Apostolic Remnant

Fulfilled prophecy identifies the Two Witnesses as the Spirit-empowered witnessing body of the early Church during the final 3.5 years leading up to AD 70. Just as Joshua and Zerubbabel stood by the "Lord of the whole earth" to build the second temple, the apostolic remnant stood in the power of the Spirit to testify against apostate Jerusalem before its fall.

The Power of the Oil

The witnesses are "two" because the Law required two for a valid legal testimony (Deuteronomy 19:15). Their "death" and "resurrection" in the streets of the city where their Lord was crucified (Jerusalem) symbolize the temporary apparent victory of the persecutors, followed by the definitive vindication of the Church when the Old Covenant system was finally removed.
