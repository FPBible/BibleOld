Resurrection Framework

From Death in Adam to Life in Christ

What This Section Is (Read First)
This section does not contain prophecy. It contains apostolic explanation. These texts define what “resurrection” means after Christ’s victory and after the covenant transition announced in the prophets.

Why This Framework Exists

The Bible uses resurrection language in multiple ways:

  • national restoration (Ezekiel 37)
  • covenant transition (Daniel, Isaiah)
  • corporate identity change (Paul)
  • life-in-the-Spirit language (New Covenant)

Without a clear interpretive framework, resurrection language is routinely flattened into future biological expectation — a category mistake.

Governing Principle
Where the apostles explain resurrection, their explanation governs how prophetic imagery is read.

Key Interpretive Texts

  • 1 Corinthians 15 — Resurrection and Victory
    The definitive apostolic explanation of resurrection: corporate, covenantal, and Christ-centred.

How This Section Relates to the Prophecy Hub

The Prophecy Hub announces:

  • judgment on the old covenant world
  • the collapse of death-bound systems
  • the establishment of Christ’s reign

The Resurrection Framework explains:

  • what it means to be raised with Christ
  • how death is defeated covenantally
  • why resurrection is present reality in the New Creation
Important Boundary
This section must not be read futuristically. Paul is not predicting resurrection. He is explaining its meaning and effects in light of Christ’s finished work.

Reading Guidance

  • Read prophecy first to understand the transition
  • Read resurrection texts to understand the outcome
  • Do not reverse the order
  • Do not import “not yet” where Paul says “now”

Resurrection is not an event believers are waiting for. It is the condition believers live from.
