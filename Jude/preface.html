Preface to Jude

The Overlap Emergency • The "Once for All" Verdict

1. The Tactical Urgency

Jude is not a theological reflection written from a peaceful ivory tower. It is an Emergency Broadcast. Writing in the mid-to-late AD 60s, Jude identifies a specific infiltration of "ungodly men" (v. 4) who were attempting to derail the New Creation by dragging it back into the filth of the Old World.

"I was constrained to write to you exhorting you to contend earnestly for the faith which was once for all delivered..." (v. 3)

The "Faith" wasn't a developing project; it was Practical Completion. Jude's audience was being tested in the final hours of the AD 30–70 Overlap. The judgment Jude announces wasn't for a future millennium—it was for the historical eviction of those apostates in the fires of AD 70.

The Seated Verdict

Jude anchors his warnings in historical examples: Egypt, the rebellious Angels, and Sodom. These aren't just stories; they are legal precedents. Just as Sodom was physically and eternally removed, the "Sodom" of Jude's day (Apostate Jerusalem, Rev 11:8) was facing the same **Seated Verdict** of the King.
