Fleeing Spiritual Babylon

The Call to Escape

Zechariah 2:7 commands: "Come, Zion! Escape, you who dwell with the daughter of Babylon." While this had immediate historical weight for exiles in Persia, the language of "Zion" dwelling in "Babylon" creates a prophetic paradox. It suggests that God's people can be trapped within a system that has become spiritually identical to the ancient enemy.

The Great Harlot

In the Book of Revelation, "Babylon the Great" is described as the city that shed the blood of the prophets and saints. Jesus explicitly identified this city as first-century Jerusalem (Matthew 23:35-37). Earthly Jerusalem, which had once been the "faithful city," had become a Harlot by rejecting the Messiah and persecuting His Church. Therefore, she inherited the title and the doom of "Babylon."

The Final Flight

Just as Zechariah called the remnant to flee Babylon, Jesus warned His disciples to flee Judea when they saw the "abomination of desolation" (Matthew 24:15-16). In Revelation 18:4, the cry is repeated: "Come out of her, my people." The early Christians heeded this warning, fleeing to Pella before the Roman siege of AD 70. This was the final, historical fulfillment of escaping the "daughter of Babylon" to enter the secure, spiritual Zion of the New Covenant.
