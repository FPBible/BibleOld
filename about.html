About FPBible

FPBible is a reader-first Bible library using the World English Bible (WEB) as base text, with commentary anchored in the fulfilled covenant-transition horizon (AD 30–70) and the lived reality of the New Creation.

Start Reading

Why This Exists

A lot of modern Bible reading gets hijacked by speculation, postponement, and “not yet” delay theology. FPBible is built to do the opposite: to keep Scripture in its stated time frame, and to land it in real life now—presence, access, identity, and Spirit-led living.

If you want the simplest on-ramp: start at the Home page “Start Here” section and read the four anchors.

How to Read FPBible Pages

Step 1

Verify the base text

Each chapter is built around the WEB text. Where a chapter includes a Scripture (WEB) link, use it to confirm the passage directly.

Step 2

Read the Fulfillment Notes

These are short, direct “what this means in its covenant horizon” statements—kept tight on purpose. They set the frame before you drop into deeper commentary.

Step 3

Work through the expanded commentary

This is where the heavy lifting happens: audience relevance, covenant imagery, time statements, and the actual flow of the argument.

Step 4

Use the Prophecy Hub when terms repeat

When you hit “coming / wrath / fire / resurrection / heaven & earth / clouds,” the Prophecy Hub centralizes those explanations so chapters stay readable.

What FPBible Is / What It Isn’t

Is

A Bible library you can actually read

Clean layout, stable navigation, and consistent structure across books—so readers don’t have to “learn a new format” every chapter.

Is

Fulfilled covenant-transition commentary

The commentary is anchored in the first-century horizon (AD 30–70), and lands in present New Creation life rather than postponement.

Is

A resource designed to stay free

FPBible is built for public access—simple links, no paywalls, and shareable pages.

Isn’t

A debate platform

The goal isn’t endless argument. The goal is clarity, Scripture integrity, and a coherent covenant reading that stays inside time statements.

Isn’t

Speculation or “countdown theology”

FPBible avoids futurist drift and avoids turning apocalyptic language into modern newspaper timelines.

Isn’t

A replacement for reading Scripture

This is a study layer meant to serve Scripture, not compete with it.

Quick Answers

Why are some books missing?

Where do I start if I’m new?

Use the Home page “Start Here.” Don’t start with a random prophecy passage. Get the four anchors first, then read a finished book end-to-end.
