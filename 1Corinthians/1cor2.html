1 Corinthians 2

The Wisdom Already Revealed by the Spirit

Original text: 1 Corinthians 2 — WEB

Fulfilled Prophecy

Paul’s Message Was Not Performance (2:1–5)

When I came to you, brothers, I did not arrive with impressive speech or philosophical skill, announcing God’s testimony as a performance.

I made a deliberate decision among you: to know nothing except Jesus Christ — and Him crucified.

I was with you in weakness, fear, and much trembling. My speech and proclamation were not shaped by persuasive techniques, but by the Spirit’s active demonstration of power,

so that your trust would not rest on human wisdom, but on God’s power already at work.

God’s Wisdom Was Hidden — and Is Now Revealed (2:6–9)

We do speak wisdom — but not the wisdom of this age, nor of the rulers of this age who are being brought to nothing.

Instead, we speak God’s wisdom in a mystery: the wisdom God had prepared before the ages for our glory —

a wisdom none of the rulers of this age understood. If they had recognised it, they would not have crucified the Lord of glory.

As Scripture says: “What no eye saw, what no ear heard, what never entered the human heart — these God has now prepared for those who love Him.”

The Spirit Has Already Made This Known (2:10–13)

God has revealed these things to us through the Spirit. The Spirit searches everything — even the depths of God.

Just as no one knows a person’s thoughts except the person’s own spirit, so no one knows God’s thoughts except God’s Spirit.

We have not received the spirit of the world, but the Spirit who comes from God, so that we may know what God has freely given us.

We speak about these things, not with words taught by human wisdom, but with words taught by the Spirit, interpreting spiritual realities to those who live by the Spirit.

The Spiritual and the Natural Mind (2:14–16)

The natural person does not accept what comes from God’s Spirit. To them it appears foolish, and they cannot recognise it, because it is spiritually discerned.

The spiritual person discerns everything, yet is not subject to the world’s judgments.

“For who has known the mind of the Lord, so as to instruct Him?” But we already share the mind of Christ.

Immediate Context

After exposing factional pride in chapter 1, Paul now dismantles the Corinthian obsession with rhetorical brilliance and philosophical hierarchy. The issue is not intelligence — it is the **source of authority**. Corinth wants prestige. Paul insists on revelation.

Fulfillment Commentary

1) God’s wisdom is no longer hidden

Paul speaks of “mystery” language (2:7), but he immediately says it has now been revealed (2:10). Paul speaks of this as present and active, because Paul’s entire argument depends on revelation already given, not information waiting to arrive later.

2) “Rulers of this age” are already collapsing

Paul describes the rulers of “this age” as those who are being brought to nothing (2:6). This is covenant-age language, not a prediction about the end of the universe. Their authority was already exposed by the crucifixion itself.

3) “Prepared for our glory” is not postponed

Paul does not say glory will eventually come. He speaks of wisdom prepared for “our glory” — a shared participation in Christ’s life now revealed through the Spirit. This wisdom belongs to that realised horizon.

4) Discernment is relational, not elitist

The “spiritual person” is not a superior class. It simply describes someone participating in the Spirit’s life, as opposed to operating from the old identity structures of the age that is passing.

Key Language Notes

  • “This age” (2:6): covenant system and authority structure, not the physical cosmos.
  • “Mystery” (2:7): something once hidden but now revealed in Christ.
  • “Mind of Christ” (2:16): shared perception shaped by the cross, not private intuition.

New Creation Reality

New Creation wisdom does not reward cleverness. It reshapes perception. To live by the Spirit is to see reality through the cross, where power looks like self-giving love and wisdom looks like humility.

Reflection

Where am I still trusting impressive ideas or voices, instead of living from what God has already revealed in Christ?
