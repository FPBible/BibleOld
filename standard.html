The Fulfilled Prophecy Bible — Locked Standard

Non-Negotiable Format for Every Chapter Page

This page defines the project standard for the Fulfilled Prophecy Bible. If a chapter page breaks this standard, it is considered a fail and must be corrected. The goal is consistency, clarity, and a fulfilled / New Creation reading that does not drift.

Project scope: Scripture is presented as World English Bible (WEB) text, with Fulfillment Notes and Study Commentary (AD 30–70 covenant transition lens).

1) Chapter Header (Required)

  • Book + Chapter title (clear and descriptive)
  • One-line fulfilled theme statement (no fluff)
Rule: The theme line must signal fulfilled meaning (present reality), not distant speculation.

2) Scripture Text (WEB) — Primary Text

  • World English Bible (WEB) text is displayed as the primary text block
  • Keep formatting readable (paragraph flow is preferred over choppy fragments)
  • Do not edit the Scripture text
  • Optional: include a small “Text source: WEB” line
Rule: The Fulfilled Prophecy Bible is not a new translation. The value-add is notes + Study Commentary, not rewritten Scripture.

3) Fulfillment Notes (Required — Brief)

Immediately after the Scripture text, include 3–5 short “Fulfillment Notes.” These must be one line each—no mini-commentary.

  • Audience relevance (“this generation,” near/soon language, apostolic horizon)
  • Prophetic idiom (covenantal “heaven and earth,” clouds, fire, etc.)
  • First-century resolution (AD 30–70 covenant transition)
Fail condition: Missing notes, or notes that become paragraphs = FAIL.

4) Immediate Context Block (Required)

A short, direct paragraph answering:

  • Who is speaking
  • Who is being addressed
  • What covenant moment this is
  • Why it mattered then
Rule: Context comes before theology. Always.

5) Study Commentary (The Spine)

This is the required interpretive layer. It must explain:

  • What the text meant in its original setting
  • How it resolves in the first-century covenant transition (AD 30–70)
  • What ended (law/temple/priesthood/age/condemnation systems)
  • What replaced it (presence/Spirit/New Creation life in Christ)
Rule: No “already / not yet.” Say what ended. Say what began.

6) Prophecy Hub Links (Mandatory When Prophetic)

If the chapter includes any of the following, a Prophecy Hub link is required:

  • Judgment language
  • Coming / clouds / day of the Lord language
  • Resurrection language
  • Heaven and earth / cosmic collapse language
  • Kingdom transfer / enthronement language
Fail condition: Prophetic tone with no Prophecy Hub link = FAIL.

7) Cross-References (Curated, Not Dumped)

  • 3–8 max
  • Chosen for clarity, not volume
  • Include: OT source / NT fulfillment / one epistle support
Rule: Every cross-reference must clarify the text, not impress the reader.

8) Key Idioms & Language Notes (Only When Relevant)

Short explanations only. No lexicon essays. Examples:

  • Heaven and earth
  • Coming on the clouds
  • That day
  • Wrath / fire
  • Resurrection
  • World / age (aiōn)
  • Land (gē)
Rule: Add language notes only when the phrase is actually present or implied in the chapter.

9) New Creation Reality (Today)

Answer the real question: “So what does this mean now?” Focus on:

  • Identity
  • Union
  • Presence
  • Freedom from condemnation
  • Life in the Spirit
Rule: No moralism. No fear. No end-times anxiety.

10) Reflection Prompt

One grounded question. It must be:

  • Not guilt-based
  • Not performance-based
  • Presence-based
Rule: Keep it short and honest. One question only.

11) Navigation & Consistency (Required)

Every chapter page must include consistent navigation at both top and bottom:

  • Index
  • Previous
  • Next
  • Top anchor
  • Prophecy Hub link (book level or chapter level — but consistent)
Fail condition: Missing nav, broken nav, or inconsistent layout = FAIL.

Pass / Fail Summary

A chapter FAILS if:

  • It sounds future-oriented without clear fulfilled clarification
  • It lacks fulfillment explanation
  • It lacks Prophecy Hub linkage where prophetic language is present
  • It skips commentary
  • It breaks layout consistency

A chapter PASSES if:

  • Fulfillment is unmistakable
  • Context is clear
  • Language is modern and clean
  • Navigation and structure are consistent across the book
