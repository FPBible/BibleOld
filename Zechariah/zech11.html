Zechariah 11

The Rejected Shepherd and the Breaking of the Covenant

1. Immediate Context

Following the soaring promises of the New Exodus and the ingathering of the flock, Zechariah 11 delivers a devastating prophetic reality check. Before the spiritual Kingdom can be fully established, the physical nation of Israel must face its ultimate covenantal crisis. This chapter is one of the most vividly fulfilled prophetic texts in the entire Bible. It details the arrival of the True Shepherd, His tragic rejection and valuation at a mere thirty pieces of silver, and the catastrophic consequences that followed. God graphically portrays the severance of the Old Covenant system through the breaking of two staffs—Favor and Union—culminating in the fiery destruction of the temple and the horrific civil war that consumed Jerusalem in AD 70.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/ZEC11.htm

1 Open your doors, Lebanon, that the fire may devour your cedars.
2 Wail, cypress tree, for the cedar has fallen, because the glorious ones are destroyed! Wail, you oaks of Bashan, for the strong forest has come down!
3 A voice of the wailing of the shepherds! For their glory is destroyed: a voice of the roaring of young lions! For the pride of the Jordan is ruined.
4 Yahweh my God says: "Feed the flock of slaughter.
5 Their buyers slaughter them, and go unpunished. Those who sell them say, 'Blessed be Yahweh, for I am rich;' and their own shepherds don't pity them.
6 For I will no more pity the inhabitants of the land," says Yahweh; "but, behold, I will deliver the men everyone into his neighbor's hand, and into the hand of his king. They will strike the land, and I won't deliver them out of their hand."
7 So I fed the flock of slaughter, especially the oppressed of the flock. I took to myself two staffs. The one I called "Favor", and the other I called "Union", and I fed the flock.
8 I cut off the three shepherds in one month; for my soul was weary of them, and their soul also loathed me.
9 Then I said, "I will not feed you. That which dies, let it die; and that which is to be cut off, let it be cut off; and let those who are left eat each other's flesh."
10 I took my staff Favor, and cut it apart, that I might break my covenant which I had made with all the peoples.
11 It was broken in that day; and thus the poor of the flock that listened to me knew that it was the word of Yahweh.
12 I said to them, "If you think it best, give me my wages; and if not, keep them." So they weighed for my wages thirty pieces of silver.
13 Yahweh said to me, "Throw it to the potter, the handsome price that I was valued at by them!" I took the thirty pieces of silver, and threw them to the potter, in the house of Yahweh.
14 Then I cut apart my other staff, even Union, that I might break the brotherhood between Judah and Israel.
15 Yahweh said to me, "Take for yourself yet again the equipment of a foolish shepherd.
16 For, behold, I will raise up a shepherd in the land, who will not visit those who are cut off, neither will seek those who are scattered, nor heal that which is broken, nor feed that which is sound; but he will eat the meat of the fat sheep, and will tear their hoofs in pieces.
17 Woe to the worthless shepherd who leaves the flock! The sword will be on his arm, and on his right eye. His arm will be completely withered, and his right eye will be totally blinded!"

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • The Fire in Lebanon's Cedars: Historical consensus recognizes this as a prophecy of the AD 70 burning of the temple, whose massive doors and beams were constructed of Lebanon cedar. Even the Jewish Talmud acknowledges this connection to the temple's destruction.
  • Thirty Pieces of Silver: Explicitly fulfilled in Matthew 26:15 and 27:3-10, when Judas Iscariot betrayed Jesus for this exact amount, and the money was subsequently used to buy the potter's field.
  • Eating Each Other's Flesh: A horrifying, literal fulfillment during the siege of Jerusalem in AD 70, where starvation drove the inhabitants to cannibalism (documented by Josephus), fulfilling the ultimate covenantal curse of Deuteronomy 28:53.
  • Breaking the Covenant Staff: Represents the definitive, first-century end of the Old Covenant system and God's national protection over physical Israel.

4. Annotations

  • Lebanon / Cedars / Oaks: Apocalyptic and prophetic idioms frequently used to describe the majestic temple in Jerusalem and the proud, lofty rulers of the nation.
  • Flock of Slaughter: The generation of first-century Jews marked out for divine judgment because of their rejection of the Messiah and alignment with corrupt leaders.
  • Staff of Favor (Grace): Symbolizes God's covenantal boundary of protection that restrained the surrounding nations from destroying Israel.
  • Staff of Union (Bonds): Symbolizes the internal brotherhood and national solidarity of the Jewish people, which was shattered during the Jewish War.
  • Thirty Pieces of Silver: According to Exodus 21:32, this was the legal compensation price for a dead slave who had been gored by an ox—an intended, profound insult to the True Shepherd.
  • Worthless Shepherd: Represents the false messiahs and violent zealot leaders who hijacked the nation after they rejected Christ, leading the flock into total annihilation.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

The Fire in the Cedars of Lebanon (Verses 1-3)

The chapter opens with a chilling command: "Open your doors, Lebanon, that the fire may devour your cedars." To the ancient Jew, the "cedars of Lebanon" were synonymous with the glorious temple built by Solomon and later expanded by Herod, which was heavily constructed with imported cedar wood. This is an explicit prophecy of the temple's doom.

In fulfilled eschatology, this text is a crucial anchor. The Jewish Talmud (Yoma 39b) records an astonishing historical event: forty years before the destruction of the temple (exactly aligning with the crucifixion of Christ), the massive temple doors mysteriously swung open by themselves. Rabbi Johanan ben Zakkai reportedly rebuked the doors, quoting this exact verse from Zechariah 11:1, recognizing it as the omen of the temple's impending fiery destruction in AD 70. The "wailing of the shepherds" was the mourning of the apostate priesthood as their glory was reduced to ashes by the Roman legions.

Pastoring the Flock Doomed to Slaughter (Verses 4-6)

God commands the prophet to act out the role of a shepherd feeding a "flock of slaughter." This flock is exploited by their buyers and sellers—the corrupt religious and political elite who enrich themselves while maintaining a veneer of piety ("Blessed be Yahweh, for I am rich"). God declares He will no longer pity the land, but will deliver them into the hand of "his king" (Caesar) and into their neighbors' hands (civil war).

When Jesus entered His public ministry, He stepped perfectly into this role. He was the Good Shepherd sent to the lost sheep of the house of Israel. However, He knew He was ministering to a generation marked for slaughter. He warned them that unless they repented, they would all likewise perish (Luke 13:3). The corrupt Pharisees and Sadducees had sold out the nation, eventually declaring, "We have no king but Caesar" (John 19:15). God granted their wish, delivering them entirely into Caesar's destructive hand in AD 70.

The Breaking of Covenant Favor (Verses 7-11)

The Shepherd takes two staffs to guide the flock: Favor (Grace/Protection) and Union (Brotherhood). Despite His care, the sheep loathe Him, and His soul becomes weary of their profound rebellion. He makes a terrifying declaration of abandonment: "I will not feed you. That which dies, let it die... and let those who are left eat each other's flesh." He then breaks the staff named Favor.

The breaking of this staff signifies the annulment of the Old Covenant system and the removal of God's hedge of protection over physical Israel. When Jesus pronounced His final woes upon Jerusalem in Matthew 23, He declared their house desolate. The grace that had restrained the nations was snapped. Only the "poor of the flock"—the believing Jewish remnant who listened to the Shepherd and fled to Pella before the siege—knew that this catastrophic shift was the definitive word of Yahweh coming to pass.

The Ultimate Insult: Thirty Pieces of Silver (Verses 12-13)

In one of the most stunningly precise prophecies in Scripture, the Shepherd asks for His wages, offering them the chance to value His ministry. They weigh out thirty pieces of silver. Under the Mosaic Law (Exodus 21:32), this was not a royal salary; it was the exact compensation price paid for a dead, gored slave. It was a calculated insult. Yahweh tells the Shepherd to throw this "handsome price" to the potter in the house of the Lord.

History bears witness to the flawless fulfillment of this text. Judas Iscariot conspired with the chief priests to betray Jesus for exactly thirty pieces of silver (Matthew 26:15). Gripped by remorse, Judas later threw the silver into the temple sanctuary (the house of Yahweh). The priests, unwilling to put blood money in the treasury, used it to purchase the potter's field (Matthew 27:3-10). The apostate leadership valued the Lord of Glory at the price of dead property, sealing their own covenantal doom.

The Broken Brotherhood and the Worthless Shepherd (Verses 14-17)

Following the rejection of the True Shepherd, the second staff, Union, is snapped, breaking the brotherhood between Judah and Israel. God then promises to raise up a "worthless shepherd" who will tear the flock to pieces instead of healing them.

During the Roman siege of AD 67-70, the horrors within the walls of Jerusalem were worse than the armies outside. The staff of Union was broken as rival zealot factions (led by false messiahs and worthless shepherds like John of Gischala and Simon bar Giora) butchered each other, burned their own food supplies, and plunged the city into absolute madness and literal cannibalism. By rejecting the Prince of Peace, they were handed over to the worthless shepherds of violence, resulting in the complete and total end of the Old Covenant age.

8. New Creation Reality

The Old Covenant flock was handed over to devastation because they valued the world's power over the gentle leading of God. The New Creation reality is that you belong to the True Shepherd who, despite being valued as nothing by the world, paid the ultimate price to securely guide, heal, and feed you eternally in His Kingdom.

9. Reflection

When the culture around you completely undervalues the truth of Christ (treating it like "thirty pieces of silver"), how does knowing the historical vindication of the Good Shepherd strengthen your resolve to follow Him?
