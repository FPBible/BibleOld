Daniel 12

The Time of Trouble • The Shattering of the Holy People • The Sealed Book

1. Immediate Context

The sweeping historical vision given to Daniel by the heavenly messenger reaches its terrifying but glorious conclusion. Continuing uninterrupted from Chapter 11, the prophecy lands squarely in the first century, during the dominance of the Roman Empire.

The angel describes an unprecedented "time of trouble" that will engulf the nation of Israel. During this tribulation, God will deliver His faithful remnant (those written in the book), and the long-awaited resurrection will occur. Most importantly, Daniel explicitly asks *when* all these things will be finished. The angel answers with an unbending historical timestamp: it will all happen when the power of the holy people is shattered once and for all.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/DAN12.htm

1 “At that time Michael will stand up, the great prince who stands for the children of your people; and there will be a time of trouble, such as never was since there was a nation even to that same time. At that time your people will be delivered, everyone who is found written in the book.
2 Many of those who sleep in the dust of the earth will awake, some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt.
3 Those who are wise will shine as the brightness of the expanse. Those who turn many to righteousness will shine as the stars forever and ever.
4 But you, Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end. Many will run back and forth, and knowledge will be increased.”
5 Then I, Daniel, looked, and behold, two others stood, one on the river bank on this side, and the other on the river bank on that side.
6 One said to the man clothed in linen, who was above the waters of the river, “How long will it be to the end of these wonders?”
7 I heard the man clothed in linen, who was above the waters of the river, when he held up his right hand and his left hand to heaven, and swore by him who lives forever that it will be for a time, times, and a half; and when they have made an end of breaking in pieces the power of the holy people, all these things will be finished.
8 I heard, but I didn’t understand. Then I said, “My lord, what will be the outcome of these things?”
9 He said, “Go your way, Daniel; for the words are shut up and sealed until the time of the end.
10 Many will purify themselves, and make themselves white, and be refined; but the wicked will do wickedly. None of the wicked will understand, but the wise will understand.
11 “From the time that the continual burnt offering is taken away, and the abomination that makes desolate set up, there will be one thousand two hundred ninety days.
12 Blessed is he who waits, and comes to the one thousand three hundred thirty-five days.
13 “But go your way until the end; for you will rest, and will stand in your inheritance at the end of the days.”

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • The Time of Trouble (v. 1): Jesus quotes this exact verse in Matthew 24:21: "For then there will be great tribulation, such as has not been from the beginning of the world until now, no, nor ever will be." Jesus specifically applied this to the impending Roman siege of Jerusalem, placing Daniel 12 definitively in the first century AD.
  • The Definitive Timestamp (v. 7): The angel swears by God that *all these things* (the tribulation, the deliverance, the resurrection) will be finished "when they have made an end of breaking in pieces the power of the holy people." The physical, political, and covenantal power of the Old Covenant Jewish nation was permanently shattered in AD 70 with the destruction of the Temple.
  • Sealing the Book (v. 4): Once again, Daniel is told to seal the book because the "time of the end" (the end of the Jewish age in AD 70) was nearly 600 years away. Contrast this with Revelation 22:10, where John is told *not* to seal his book because the time was at hand.

4. Annotations

  • Michael will stand up — The archangel assigned to protect the remnant of Israel. When the great tribulation hit Judea, the faithful Jewish-Christians were supernaturally protected and delivered (fleeing to Pella before the siege).
  • written in the book — The Book of Life. This echoes through Revelation; those who belonged to the New Covenant in Christ were spared from the covenant wrath poured out on apostate Jerusalem.
  • sleep in the dust of the earth — In biblical prophecy, "dust" is often a metaphor for covenant death, exile, and humiliation (Isaiah 52:2 says "Shake yourself from the dust, arise, and sit down, Jerusalem"). This refers to the great spiritual and covenantal resurrection: passing from the death of the Old Covenant into the everlasting life of the New Creation.
  • time, times, and a half — 3.5 years (or 1,260 days/42 months). The historical length of the Jewish-Roman war that crushed Jerusalem (Spring AD 67 to Fall AD 70).

5. Fulfillment Commentary

1) The Greatest Tribulation (v. 1)

Futurists teach that the "Great Tribulation" is a future, global nuclear holocaust. But biblical prophecy is deeply covenantal. The "time of trouble such as never was" was specifically directed at Daniel's people (first-century Israel) for their rejection of the Messiah and their persecution of His apostles.

Jesus perfectly anchored Daniel 12 to His own generation. In the Olivet Discourse, He warned His disciples that when they saw the Roman armies surrounding Jerusalem, they must flee to the mountains, because *that* event would be the unparalleled tribulation Daniel prophesied (Matthew 24:15-21). History confirms this: the siege of Jerusalem in AD 70 was a localized horror of starvation, crucifixion, and slaughter that ended the biblical nation of Israel forever.

2) The Covenantal Resurrection (vv. 2–3)

Verse 2 is often read through a modern, Greek-philosophical lens as the biological reanimation of physical corpses at the end of the space-time universe. But apocalyptic literature uses the imagery of death and resurrection to describe *covenantal status*.

Ezekiel's vision of the "Valley of Dry Bones" (Ezek 37) was not about biology; it was about God restoring a dead, exiled nation. In the New Testament, Jesus said, "He who hears my word... has passed out of death into life. The hour comes, and now is, when the dead will hear the Son of God's voice; and those who hear will live" (John 5:24-25).

At the end of the Old Covenant age, those who "slept in the dust" (the faithful saints waiting in Hades/Sheol) were vindicated and brought into the full presence of God (everlasting life), while the persecuting, apostate nation was resurrected to the shame of everlasting contempt—cast out of the Kingdom forever.

3) The Shattering of the Holy People (v. 7)

This verse is the deathblow to futurism. The heavenly figure raises both hands and swears an oath regarding the timeline. He does not say, "These things will be finished when the world burns up." He says they will be finished "when they have made an end of breaking in pieces the power of the holy people."

The "holy people" in the Old Testament context were the physical descendants of Abraham under the Mosaic Law. Their "power" was entirely wrapped up in the Temple, the priesthood, and the sacrificial system. In AD 70, the Roman legions completely shattered that power, literally leaving not one stone upon another. According to the angel's oath, all the events of Daniel 12—including the Great Tribulation and the Resurrection—were finished when the Temple fell.

4) The Time of the End (vv. 8–13)

Daniel admits he doesn't fully understand. He asks, "What will be the outcome of these things?" The angel essentially tells him, "Go rest, Daniel. This is not for your lifetime. The book is sealed until the time of the end."

Daniel died in exile, resting in the promise that he would stand in his inheritance at the end of the days. Six centuries later, the Apostle Peter wrote to the first-century believers, revealing that the Old Testament prophets "sought and searched diligently" about these exact prophecies. Peter declared that it was revealed to them that "not to themselves, but to us, they ministered these things" (1 Peter 1:10-12). The "time of the end" was the first century, and Daniel is currently standing in his glorious inheritance in the New Creation.

8. New Creation Reality

You are not waiting for a future tribulation, and you do not need to fear the "time of trouble." The power of the old, shadowy system was definitively shattered in AD 70, fully establishing the New Covenant. You are reading covenant history for assurance, not headlines for doom. The book is unsealed. The resurrection life of Christ is your present possession, and you already stand in your everlasting inheritance alongside Daniel and all the faithful saints.
