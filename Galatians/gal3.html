Galatians 3

1. Immediate Context

Having established the "horizontal" vindication of his Gospel in Chapter 2, Paul now turns back to the Galatians with a piercing rebuke. He moves from history to theology, dismantling the logic that says the Law is the "completion" of faith. By appealing to the Spirit-experience of the Galatians and the pre-Law covenant with Abraham, Paul proves that the Mosaic economy was a strictly temporary, protective parenthesis in the overarching story of the Promise.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/GAL03.htm

1 Foolish Galatians, who has bewitched you not to obey the truth, before whose eyes Jesus Christ was openly portrayed among you as crucified?
2 I just want to learn this from you: Did you receive the Spirit by the works of the law, or by hearing of faith?
3 Are you so foolish? Having begun in the Spirit, are you now completed in the flesh?
4 Did you suffer so many things in vain, if it is indeed in vain?
5 He therefore who supplies the Spirit to you and does miracles among you, does he do it by the works of the law, or by hearing of faith?
6 Even as Abraham “believed God, and it was counted to him for righteousness.”
7 Know therefore that those who are of faith are children of Abraham.
8 The Scripture, foreseeing that God would justify the Gentiles by faith, preached the Good News beforehand to Abraham, saying, “In you all the nations will be blessed.”
9 So then, those who are of faith are blessed with the faithful Abraham.
10 For as many as are of the works of the law are under a curse. For it is written, “Cursed is everyone who doesn’t continue in all things that are written in the book of the law, to do them.”
11 Now that no man is justified by the law before God is evident, for, “The righteous will live by faith.”
12 The law is not of faith, but, “The man who does them will live by them.”
13 Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law, having become a curse for us. For it is written, “Cursed is everyone who hangs on a tree,”
14 that the blessing of Abraham might come on the Gentiles through Christ Jesus, that we might receive the promise of the Spirit through faith.
15 Brothers, I speak like men. Though it is only a man’s covenant, yet when it has been confirmed, no one makes it void or adds to it.
16 Now the promises were spoken to Abraham and to his seed. He doesn’t say, “To seeds,” as of many, but as of one, “To your seed,” which is Christ.
17 Now I say this: A covenant confirmed beforehand by God in Christ, the law, which came four hundred thirty years after, does not annul, so as to make the promise of no effect.
18 For if the inheritance is of the law, it is no more of promise; but God has granted it to Abraham by promise.
19 Why then the law? It was added because of transgressions, until the seed should come to whom the promise has been made. It was ordained through angels by the hand of a mediator.
20 Now a mediator is not between one, but God is one.
21 Is the law then against the promises of God? Certainly not! For if there had been a law given which could make alive, most certainly righteousness would have been of the law.
22 But the Scripture imprisoned all things under sin, that the promise by faith in Jesus Christ might be given to those who believe.
23 But before faith came, we were kept in custody under the law, confined for the faith which should afterwards be revealed.
24 So that the law has become our tutor to bring us to Christ, that we might be justified by faith.
25 But now that faith has come, we are no longer under a tutor.
26 For you are all children of God, through faith in Christ Jesus.
27 For as many of you as were baptized into Christ have put on Christ.
28 There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free man, there is neither male nor female; for you are all one in Christ Jesus.
29 If you are Christ’s, then you are Abraham’s seed and heirs according to promise.

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • Pre-Law Priority: The promise to Abraham is the original covenant, established 430 years before Sinai (v. 17).
  • Temporal Boundary: The Law was added strictly "until the seed should come" (v. 19).
  • Covenant Transition: The shift from "under a tutor" to "sons of God" marks the historical transition of the terminal generation (v. 23-26).
  • Inheritance Marker: The "blessing of Abraham" is defined as the reception of the promised Spirit, not the possession of a physical land (v. 14).

4. Annotations

  • Bewitched (Baskaino): Verse 1. To fascinate or lead astray by a deceptive spell; specifically the spell of religious performance.
  • Seed: Verse 16. A corporate and singular identity. While physical Israel was "seeds," the singular "Seed" is Christ, through whom the promise flows.
  • Paidagogos (Tutor/Guardian): Verse 24. A household servant who disciplined and supervised a minor until they reached maturity.
  • Put on Christ: Verse 27. A definitive identity shift through baptism/union, replacing ethnic and social clothing.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

The Experience of the Spirit vs. The Flesh (v. 1-5)

Paul begins with a sharp appeal to the Galatians' own history. He asks: Did God supply the Spirit and miracles because of your Law-observance or because of faith? This is a foundational "data point" in fulfilled eschatology. The Spirit was the universally recognized marker of the New Covenant's arrival. If the Spirit arrived *without* the Law, then the Law cannot be the mechanism that completes or sustains the Spirit's work. To think that the "flesh" (external rules) could "finish" what the Spirit started is, to Paul, the height of theological folly.

Abraham: The Blueprint for Justifying the Nations (v. 6-9)

Paul reaches behind Moses to Abraham to prove that the "faith-method" is the original method. Abraham was justified by trust centuries before the Law even existed. Paul makes a startling claim: the Scripture "preached the Good News beforehand to Abraham." This means the Gospel is not a new invention or a workaround for a failed Law; it is the fulfillment of the primary covenant. Those who live by faith are not "new" children of Abraham; they are the true ones, regardless of their ethnic lineage.

The Redemptive Swap: Curse for Blessing (v. 10-14)

The Law carries a built-in curse for any lack of perfect compliance. Paul argues that Christ "redeemed us from the curse" by becoming a curse Himself. This is a covenantal transaction. By dying on the "tree," Christ took the specific judicial penalty of the Mosaic economy upon Himself to release those held under it. The goal was to let the "blessing of Abraham"—the Spirit—bypass the Law-barrier and reach the nations. The cross, therefore, is the legal gateway out of the old system and into the New Creation.

The Law as a Parenthesis (v. 15-18)

Paul uses a legal analogy: a confirmed human covenant cannot be annulled by a later document. The Law, arriving 430 years later, cannot overwrite the Promise. In the timeline of fulfillment, the Law is a parenthesis, not the final chapter. It was never intended to grant inheritance; it was intended to bridge the gap until the "Seed" (Christ) arrived. If the inheritance came through the Law, the original Promise to Abraham would have been a lie.

The Role of the Guardian (v. 19-25)

If the Law doesn't give life, why was it given? Paul says it was "added because of transgressions." It was a "tutor" or guardian (paidagogos) meant to keep the people in custody until "the coming faith would be revealed." A tutor is never the child's equal and never the final authority; their job is strictly temporary. Once Christ (the Heir) arrived, the tutor's jurisdiction ended. For the first-century church, the tutor's school was closing, and it was time to step into the mature sonship of the New Covenant.

The Universal Heirship (v. 26-29)

The chapter closes with the total dissolution of Old Covenant social and ethnic categories. In Christ, there is no longer "Jew or Greek, slave or free." This is not an erasure of diversity, but an erasure of *distinction in status*. Through union with Christ (being baptized into Him), believers "put on Christ" as their singular identity. If you belong to the Seed, you are an heir to the original Promise. The New Creation has arrived, and the old boundary markers have no authority over the inheritance.

8. New Creation Reality

Galatians 3 reminds us that the Spirit within you is the only credential that matters. You are not a "second-class" citizen waiting to be completed by a deeper religious ritual or a stricter moral code. You are already a son and an heir because you are in the Seed. If your faith has become a "striving to finish in the flesh," you have been bewitched by the old world. New Creation life is the rest of knowing that the Promise was made to Christ, and since you are in Him, the inheritance is yours by right of union, not right of performance.

9. Reflection

Am I trying to "finish" by my own effort what the Spirit has already completed in Christ?
