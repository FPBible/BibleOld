Matthew 28 — Resurrection, Authority, and the Mission Completed

Immediate Context

Matthew 28 concludes the Gospel by revealing the Father’s public vindication of Jesus, the collapse of the old guard’s authority, and the commissioning of the New Covenant people as the Spirit-filled witnesses who would carry the Kingdom through the first-century transition until the Mosaic age was fully brought to its end.

Scripture (WEB)

1Now after the Sabbath, as it began to dawn on the first day of the week, Mary Magdalene and the other Mary came to see the tomb. 2Behold, there was a great earthquake, for an angel of the Lord descended from the sky, and came and rolled away the stone from the door, and sat on it. 3His appearance was like lightning, and his clothing white as snow. 4For fear of him, the guards shook, and became like dead men.

5The angel answered the women, “Don’t be afraid, for I know that you seek Jesus, who has been crucified. 6He is not here, for he has risen, just like he said. Come, see the place where the Lord was lying. 7Go quickly and tell his disciples, ‘He has risen from the dead, and behold, he goes before you into Galilee; there you will see him.’ Behold, I have told you.”

8They departed quickly from the tomb with fear and great joy, and ran to bring his disciples word. 9As they went to tell his disciples, behold, Jesus met them, saying, “Rejoice!” They came and took hold of his feet, and worshiped him. 10Then Jesus said to them, “Don’t be afraid. Go tell my brothers that they should go into Galilee, and there they will see me.”

11Now while they were going, behold, some of the guards came into the city and told the chief priests all the things that had happened. 12When they were assembled with the elders, and had taken counsel, they gave a large amount of silver to the soldiers, 13saying, “Say that his disciples came by night, and stole him away while we slept. 14If this comes to the governor’s ears, we will persuade him and make you free of worry.” 15So they took the money and did as they were told. This saying was spread abroad among the Jews, and continues until today.

16But the eleven disciples went into Galilee, to the mountain where Jesus had sent them. 17When they saw him, they worshiped him, but some doubted.

18Jesus came to them and spoke to them, saying, “All authority has been given to me in heaven and on earth. 19Go, and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, 20teaching them to observe all things that I commanded you. Behold, I am with you always, even to the end of the age.”

Fulfillment Notes (STRICT)

The resurrection publicly vindicates Jesus as Messiah and Judge of the covenant order.

The false report confirms leadership resistance even after decisive divine action.

“All authority” announces completed enthronement, not a future coronation.

The mission unfolds within the first-century horizon leading to the end of the Mosaic age.

Annotations

All authority has been given to me in heaven and on earth: Total covenant authority over both Temple (“heaven”) and Land (“earth”).
I am with you always: Covenant presence replacing Temple-mediated access.
the end of the age: The conclusion of the Mosaic covenant system in AD 70.

Fulfillment Commentary

1) Resurrection as Covenant Vindication

Matthew does not present the resurrection as an abstract miracle or merely personal hope for the afterlife. It is a public, historical vindication of Jesus’ authority claims against the Temple system. The earthquake, angelic descent, and terrified guards all signal that the old guardians of power have lost control. The resurrection announces that the covenant lawsuit has been decided in heaven, and the verdict now stands awaiting its earthly outworking.

2) The Collapse of the Old Guard’s Credibility

The bribing of the guards is deliberate narrative irony. The leadership that demanded truth at trial now purchases lies to protect its collapsing authority. Matthew records this not to explain away skepticism, but to expose the moral bankruptcy of the Temple regime. The resurrection does not produce repentance in the system; it hardens it. This seals the necessity of judgment within that generation.

3) Authority Fully Transferred

Jesus’ declaration that all authority has been given to Him is not future-oriented. In Daniel 7 terms, this is the moment of enthronement. Authority over “heaven and earth” means the entire covenant structure—Temple, Law, priesthood, and land administration—is now under the Son of Man. Nothing remains pending.

4) The Mission and Its Timeframe

The Great Commission is not detached from Matthew 24. The mission unfolds before “the end of the age,” not after it. The apostles would take the gospel to the nations during the final forty-year transition period, culminating in the removal of the old order. This is why the book of Acts ends before AD 70—the mission had reached maturity.

5) Presence Replaces Temple

“I am with you always” is the final blow to Temple theology. God’s presence is no longer located, mediated, or restricted. The risen Christ dwells with His people directly. When the Temple fell, nothing was lost—because nothing essential remained there.
