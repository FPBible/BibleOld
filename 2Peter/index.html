2 Peter

The Dissolution of the Elements and the New Covenant Hope.

Book Summary

In his final letter, Peter addresses scoffers who question the timing of the Lord's parousia. He frames the coming judgment not as the destruction of the physical planet, but as the fiery dissolution of the "elements" (stoicheia)—the legalistic principles of the Old Covenant system—to make way for the New Heavens and New Earth where righteousness dwells.

Covenantal Keys for 2 Peter

  • The Elements (Stoicheia): In Peter's context, these are the elementary principles of the Law (temple, priesthood, sacrifices), not atoms or molecules.
  • Speedy Fulfillment: Peter reinforces that "the end of all things is at hand" (1 Pet 4:7), maintaining the same urgent timeframe as Jesus and Paul.
  • New Heavens & Earth: This is a covenantal state of existence (the New Jerusalem/New Covenant) rather than a replacement of the physical globe.
