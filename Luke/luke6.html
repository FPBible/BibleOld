Luke 6

The Lord of Rest • The New Foundation • The Sovereign Manifesto

1. Immediate Context

In Luke 6, the glory of the Son directly confronts the heartbeat of Old Covenant identity: the Sabbath. During the transition generation (AD 30–70), the Sabbath had been reduced to a mechanism of exhausting performance, weaponized by a religious elite to maintain control. By declaring Himself "Lord of the Sabbath," the Son asserts His jurisdictional right to redefine the very concept of time and rest. He is not merely "breaking" a rule; He is demonstrating that the Sabbath was always a shadow intended to find its substance in His presence.

The chapter moves from this legal confrontation to the formal selection of the twelve—the administrative heads of the New Covenant priesthood. Standing on a "level place," the Son delivers a manifesto that turns the old religious world upside down. He redefines blessing, woe, and social order, grounding the life of the New Creation in the indestructible bedrock of His word. As the AD 70 horizon approached, this teaching provided the "Rock" upon which the remnant would build their lives, ensuring they remained standing even as the physical temple and its religious "sand" were swept away.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/LUK06.htm

1 Now it happened on the second Sabbath after the first, that he was going through the grain fields. His disciples plucked the heads of grain, and ate, rubbing them in their hands. 2 But some of the Pharisees said to them, “Why do you do that which is not lawful to do on the Sabbath day?”
3 Jesus, answering them, said, “Have you not read what David did when he was hungry, he, and those who were with him; 4 how he entered into God’s house, and took and ate the show bread, and gave also to those who were with him, which is not lawful to eat except for the priests alone?” 5 He said to them, “The Son of Man is lord of the Sabbath.”
6 It also happened on another Sabbath that he entered into the synagogue and taught. There was a man there, and his right hand was withered. 7 The scribes and the Pharisees watched him, to see whether he would heal on the Sabbath, that they might find an accusation against him. 8 But he knew their thoughts; and he said to the man who had the withered hand, “Rise up, and stand in the middle.” He rose up and stood. 9 Then Jesus said to them, “I ask you, is it lawful on the Sabbath to do good, or to do harm? To save a life, or to destroy it?” 10 He looked around at them all, and said to the man, “Stretch out your hand.” He did, and his hand was restored as whole as the other. 11 But they were filled with madness, and discussed with one another what they might do to Jesus.
12 It happened in these days, that he went out to the mountain to pray, and he continued all night in prayer to God. 13 When it was day, he called his disciples, and from them he chose twelve, whom he also named apostles: 14 Simon, whom he also named Peter; Andrew, his brother; James; John; Philip; Bartholomew; 15 Matthew; Thomas; James, the son of Alphaeus; Simon, who was called the Zealot; 16 Judas the son of James; and Judas Iscariot, who also became a traitor.
17 He came down with them, and stood on a level place, with a great multitude of his disciples, and a great number of the people from all Judea and Jerusalem, and the sea coast of Tyre and Sidon, who came to hear him and to be healed of their diseases; 18 as well as those who were troubled by unclean spirits, and they were being healed. 19 All the multitude sought to touch him, for power went out from him and healed them all.
20 He lifted up his eyes on his disciples, and said, “Blessed are you who are poor, for yours is God’s Kingdom. 21 Blessed are you who hunger now, for you will be filled. Blessed are you who weep now, for you will laugh. 22 Blessed are you when men shall hate you, and when they shall exclude you, and shall revile you, and cast out your name as evil, for the Son of Man’s sake. 23 Rejoice in that day, and leap for joy, for behold, your reward is great in heaven, for their fathers did the same thing to the prophets.
24 But woe to you who are rich! For you have received your consolation. 25 Woe to you who are full now! For you will be hungry. Woe to you who laugh now! For you will mourn and weep. 26 Woe, when men shall speak well of you! For their fathers did the same thing to the false prophets.
27 But I tell you who hear: love your enemies, do good to those who hate you, 28 bless those who curse you, and pray for those who mistreat you. 29 To him who strikes you on the cheek, offer also the other; and from him who takes away your cloak, don’t withhold your coat also. 30 Give to everyone who asks you, and from him who takes away your goods, don’t ask them back again. 31 As you would like people to do to you, do also to them the same way. 32 If you love those who love you, what credit is that to you? For even sinners love those who love them. 33 If you do good to those who do good to you, what credit is that to you? For even sinners do the same. 34 If you lend to those from whom you hope to receive, what credit is that to you? Even sinners lend to sinners, to receive back as much. 35 But love your enemies, and do good, and lend, expecting nothing back; and your reward will be great, and you will be children of the Most High; for he is kind toward the unthankful and evil. 36 “Therefore be merciful, even as your Father is also merciful. 37 Don’t judge, and you won’t be judged. Don’t condemn, and you won’t be condemned. Release, and you will be released. 38 “Give, and it will be given to you: good measure, pressed down, shaken together, and running over, will be given to you. For with the same measure you measure it will be measured back to you.” 39 He spoke a parable to them, “Can the blind lead the blind? Won’t they both fall into a pit? 40 A disciple is not above his teacher, but everyone when he is fully trained will be like his teacher. 41 Why do you see the speck of chaff that is in your brother’s eye, but don’t consider the beam that is in your own eye? 42 Or how can you tell your brother, ‘Brother, let me remove the speck of chaff that is in your eye,’ when you yourself don’t see the beam that is in your own eye? You hypocrite! First remove the beam from your own eye, and then you can see clearly to remove the speck of chaff that is in your brother’s eye. 43 For there is no good tree that brings forth corrupt fruit, nor again a corrupt tree that brings forth good fruit. 44 For each tree is known by its own fruit. For people don’t gather figs from thorns, nor do they gather grapes from a bramble bush. 45 The good man out of the good treasure of his heart brings forth that which is good, and the evil man out of the evil treasure of his heart brings forth that which is evil; for out of the abundance of the heart, his mouth speaks. 46 “Why do you call me, ‘Lord, Lord,’ and don’t do the things which I say? 47 Everyone who comes to me, and hears my words, and does them, I will show you who he is like. 48 He is like a man building a house, who dug and went deep, and laid a foundation on the rock. When a flood arose, the stream broke against that house, and could not shake it, because it was founded on the rock. 49 But he who hears, and doesn’t do, is like a man who built a house on the earth without a foundation, against which the stream broke, and immediately it fell, and the ruin of that house was great.”

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • Lord of the Sabbath (v. 5): A definitive jurisdictional claim. The Son asserts that the Sabbath—the sign of the Mosaic Covenant—is subject to His word. This signals that the era of ritual rest is giving way to the era of established rest in His person.
  • Restore Whole (v. 10): A visual manifestation of the New Creation. Healing the "right hand" (the hand of strength and work) signifies that in His kingdom, the paralysis of the old world is legally and physically consumed by His touch.
  • Yours is the Kingdom (v. 20): Present tense. The Son does not promise a distant future; He announces a present legal possession for those the old world had marginalized.
  • The Flood (v. 48): An eschatological reference to the AD 70 judgment. The house on the "earth" (the old covenant system built on biological lineage) was destined for a ruin that would be "great."

4. Annotations

  • Show Bread (v. 4) — The bread of the Presence. Jesus uses David's legal exception to prove that the Son’s presence supersedes the technicalities of the law.
  • Twelve (v. 13) — Repossessing the tribal identity of Israel. The selection of the apostles is the formal assembly of the New Covenant administration.
  • Level Place (v. 17) — Contrast to Sinai. The kingdom is not announced from a mountain of smoke and distance, but on the level ground where humanity lives and suffers.
  • Foundation on the Rock (v. 48) — Petra. Not a stone, but a massive shelf of bedrock. This is the indestructible life of the Son and His finished decree.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

1) The Manifestation of Jurisdictional Glory

In the grain fields (Luke 6:1-5), we witness the Son unveiling His authority over the Mosaic calendar. The religious elite had turned the Sabbath into a cage of exhausting performance, but Jesus uses the example of David to demonstrate that the Sovereign's presence always overrides ceremonial restrictions. By declaring Himself "Lord of the Sabbath," He is legally foreclosing on the old interpretation of rest. He is not merely "permitted" to heal or eat; He is the source of the day itself. This act informed the transition generation that the shadow of the law was reaching its terminal point. The glory of the Son is seen in His ability to move His people from a state of seeking rest through rules to a state of being rest because He is present.

2) The Restoration of Strength: Undoing the Paralysis

The healing of the withered hand (Luke 6:6-10) is a profound legal disruption. The synagogue leaders were more concerned with the technicalities of their tradition than with the restoration of a human life. Jesus’s command to "stand in the middle" and then "stretch out your hand" was a manifestation of New Creation power in the heart of the old system. He proved that His touch consumes the decay of the curse, even on the day most sacred to the Old Covenant. This event was a visible testimony that the jurisdictional power of the Son had already superseded the ritual barriers of the synagogue. The "madness" of His opponents was the reaction of those who realized their control through legalism was being dismantled by manifest life.

3) The New Administration: The Royal Priesthood Established

The appointment of the twelve apostles (Luke 6:13-16) is the formal establishment of the New Covenant government. The Son chose twelve—a number that legally repossesses the identity of Israel—from the common ranks of humanity. This selection was not based on Levitical lineage or religious status, but on His sovereign decree. This is the birth of the Royal Priesthood. These men were chosen to witness the glory and carry the word that would define the New World. By calling them "apostles" (sent ones), the Son was signaling that the administration of the kingdom was no longer tied to a physical temple in Jerusalem, but to His own authority traveling through His people into the whole world.

4) The Bedrock of the New Creation

The closing manifesto (Luke 6:46-49) provides the definitive contrast between the two covenants. The house built on the "earth" without a foundation is the Old Covenant system—built on biological pride and the sand of human performance. The Son warned that the "flood" of AD 70 would result in its total ruin. Conversely, the life built on the "Rock"—the finished word of the Son—is indestructible. The glory of the New Creation is its permanence; it is a house that cannot be shaken. This commentary affirms that the transition was a move from the shifting sands of the law to the established bedrock of grace. To hear and do His word is to inhabit a jurisdiction that death and judgment cannot touch.

8. New Creation Reality

In Luke 6, the Son reveals that you are no longer a slave to religious technicalities. You are a citizen of Established Rest. The Sabbath is no longer a day you perform; it is the presence you inhabit. Your life is not a house on the sand of institutional loyalty; it is founded on the Petra of Christ’s finished decree. You are the "Blessed Poor" who possess the kingdom right now, regardless of the old world's evaluation. The mediation of those who "watch" to accuse has been legally bypassed. You are free to stretch out your hand, to love without expecting a return, and to live in the overflowing measure of the Father’s mercy. The flood has passed; you remain standing in His glory.
