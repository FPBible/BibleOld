The New Creation Manifesto

Moving from expectation to fulfillment. The Sun has risen. The waiting has ended.

We are not waiting for the day to arrive. The day has arrived.

The Age Has Ended, the Promise Is Kept

The “end of the age” spoken of by Jesus was not the end of the material universe. It was the consummation of the Jewish covenant world, completed in the first century with the destruction of Jerusalem in AD 70.

The Old Covenant order passed away. The New Covenant age stands established. Faith is no longer oriented toward unfinished prophecy, but toward accomplished reality.

Parousia Means Presence, Not Arrival

Parousia does not describe a brief visit or a future interruption. It signifies royal presence — authority exercised, reign established, nearness realized.

Christ is not absent. He reigns now.

The Old World of Shadows Has Passed

The former “heavens and earth” was the covenant world structured around temple, priesthood, and separation. That world has been removed.

The veil is torn. Access is no longer mediated. Barriers are gone. This is not symbolic optimism for the future — it is the present condition of life in Christ.

The Holy City Is Open

The New Jerusalem is not a future city descending from the sky. It is the living community of God’s people — a city without walls, whose gates are always open.

Healing flows now. Access is immediate. Identity is present.

How We Live in the Day

We stop waiting. We walk in awareness. We offer life.

The New Creation is not an idea to defend. It is a reality to inhabit.

We Are Children of the Day

We do not wait for Christ. We walk with Him.
