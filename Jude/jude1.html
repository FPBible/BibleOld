Jude

Contending for the Faith • The Doom of the Apostates • Once for All Delivered

Covenantal Emergency AD 70 Vindication Practical Completion

1. Immediate Context

Jude, the brother of James, writes with an intensity that matches the late AD 60s. He intended to write about "our common salvation," but the legal emergency of the **AD 30–70 Overlap** forced his hand. He writes to a community being infiltrated by Judean apostates who were attempting to drag the New Creation back into the license of the flesh and the shadows of the Old World.

His verdict is absolute: These men are "of old ordained to this condemnation" (v. 4). The judgment they faced was not a distant end-of-the-world event, but the imminent AD 70 destruction that would physically remove their operating ground.

2. Scripture (WEB)

3 Beloved, while I was very eager to write to you about our common salvation, I was constrained to write to you exhorting you to contend earnestly for the faith which was once for all delivered to the saints. 4 For there are certain men who crept in secretly, even those who were of old written of for this condemnation: ungodly men, turning the grace of our God into indecency, and denying our only Master, God, and Lord, Jesus Christ. 5 Now I desire to remind you... how that the Lord, having saved a people out of the land of Egypt, afterward destroyed those who didn’t believe. 6 Angels who didn’t keep their first domain, but deserted their own dwelling place, he has kept in everlasting bonds under darkness for the judgment of the great day. 7 Even as Sodom and Gomorrah... are set forth as an example, suffering the punishment of eternal fire... 14 About these also Enoch, the seventh from Adam, prophesied, saying, "Behold, the Lord came with ten thousands of his holy ones, 15 to execute judgment on all, and to convict all the ungodly of all their works of ungodliness..."

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • Once for All Delivered (v. 3): The Greek hapax. The contract is closed. There is no "v2.0" coming. This is the **Seated Authority**—the faith was delivered fully in the first century.
  • The Great Day (v. 6): Not a final day of biological life, but the final day of the Old Covenant age. The "Angels" (messengers/priests) who left their post are the Mosaic leadership who abandoned the true dwelling place of God for a dead stone building.
  • Sodom as Example (v. 7): Jude uses Sodom as the "antitype" for Jerusalem. In Revelation 11:8, Jerusalem is literally called "Sodom and Egypt." The fire Jude describes is the covenantal fire of AD 70.
  • Enoch's Prophecy (v. 14): Jude quotes Enoch to anchor the Parousia. The Lord "came" (aorist tense in the prophetic view) to execute judgment on *that* ungodly generation.

4. Annotations

  • crept in secretly — The "wolves in sheep's clothing" Jesus warned about (Matthew 7:15). They were the internal threat to the transition.
  • clouds without water — Promising New Creation life but delivering only the dry husks of the Old World.
  • twice dead, plucked up by the roots — They died to the Law, but rejected the Spirit; they are covenantally uprooted and have no place in the New Creation.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

1) The Legal Dispute Over the Body (v. 9)

Jude references Michael the archangel contending with the devil over the "body of Moses." In covenantal terms, the "Body of Moses" is the Mosaic system itself. The devil (the accuser) wanted to keep the "body" active to continue the ministry of condemnation. Michael's refusal to bring a "railing judgment" shows the **Seated Authority** of God: the system was being removed by divine decree, not by human debate.

2) The Mockers of the Last Time (vv. 17–19)

Jude reminds them that the Apostles said there would be mockers in the "last time." This "last time" was the final hour of the Mosaic age. These mockers weren't making fun of a "Second Coming" 2,000 years away; they were mocking the imminent manifestation of Christ's presence and the impending doom of Jerusalem.
