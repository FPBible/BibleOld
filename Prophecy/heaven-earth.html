Heaven and Earth

Covenant World Language · Not Physical Cosmology

In Scripture, “heaven and earth” is often covenant language: an ordered world of authority, worship, law, and identity. It can describe the structure of a covenant administration—who rules, how access is defined, and how the people are governed— not merely the material universe.

Key control: In prophetic contexts, “heaven and earth” frequently functions as covenant-order language (a world of worship and authority), especially in judgment/transition passages.

1) “Heaven and Earth” in Covenant Lawsuit Language

The prophets regularly call “heaven and earth” as witnesses in covenant lawsuits. This is legal-prophetic rhetoric: the covenant order is summoned to hear the indictment.

  • Isaiah 1 (WEB) — “Hear, heavens… give ear, earth…” (covenant indictment)
  • Deuteronomy 32 (WEB) — “Give ear, heavens… let the earth hear…” (covenant witness song)

These passages are not teaching astronomy. They are using the “heaven/earth” pair as covenant courtroom language. That’s why the same pair can be “shaken,” “worn out,” or “passed away” when a covenant order is judged and replaced.

2) What “Heaven” and “Earth” Often Map To

In prophetic-covenant usage, the pair commonly maps like this:

  • Heaven — the ruling order (authority, leadership, priestly/temple administration)
  • Earth — the people living under that order (the covenant community in the land)

This mapping doesn’t mean the Bible never speaks about the physical heavens and earth. It means: in prophetic judgment/transition contexts, the language often carries covenant meaning.

3) Creation / De-Creation Imagery in the Prophets

The prophets describe covenant collapse using “creation reversal” imagery: sun darkened, stars falling, heavens shaken, earth trembling. This is stock prophetic vocabulary for the fall of a ruling order.

  • Isaiah 13 (WEB) — Babylon judgment with cosmic imagery
  • Isaiah 34 (WEB) — Edom judgment with heavens/scroll imagery
  • Ezekiel 32 (WEB) — Egypt judgment with cosmic signs

None of these passages report the end of the physical universe. They use de-creation imagery to describe the end of a political/covenantal “world.”

4) Jesus and “Heaven and Earth” (Matthew 24:35)

Jesus stands in this prophetic tradition. In Matthew 24, the discourse is anchored in the temple’s judgment and locked to “this generation.” Within that covenant horizon, Jesus says:

  • Matthew 24 (WEB) — “Heaven and earth will pass away, but my words will not pass away.”

In that setting, “heaven and earth” functions as covenant-order language: the old administration (temple-centred world) is shakable and passing; Christ’s word is the fixed authority of the new order.

Context rule: Matthew 24 is not framed as cosmology. It is framed as temple judgment + covenant crisis + time-lock (“this generation”).

5) Hebrews 12: Shaking, Removal, and the Unshakable Kingdom

Hebrews 12 explicitly interprets “shaking” language. The point is not the destruction of creation, but the removal of what can be shaken so that what cannot be shaken remains.

  • Hebrews 12 (WEB) — shaking/removal → unshakable kingdom

Hebrews is temple-and-covenant argument from start to finish: priesthood, sacrifice, access, and covenant replacement. So when Hebrews speaks of shaking “heaven and earth,” it is covenant transition language: the old order removed; the kingdom received.

6) 2 Peter 3: “Heavens and Earth” and Isaiah’s New Creation Promise

2 Peter 3 is often forced into “planet incineration” readings, but Peter’s own framing points to covenant judgment and transition, and his “new heavens and new earth” expectation comes from Isaiah’s promise.

  • 2 Peter 3 (WEB)
  • Isaiah 65 (WEB) — source of “new heavens and new earth” promise
  • Isaiah 66 (WEB) — covenant outcome language in prophetic register

Isaiah’s “new heavens and new earth” is not written as an astrophysics lecture. It is covenant renewal language: a new order of life, worship, and peace under God’s reign. Peter draws on that promise to speak of the transition into the new covenant world.

Source control: “New heavens and new earth” is Isaiah’s prophetic covenant promise before it is ever Peter’s phrase.

7) What This Is NOT Saying

  • Not “the Bible never refers to physical heavens/earth.” Context determines usage.
  • Not “every occurrence is symbolic.” The claim is about prophetic/judgment/transition contexts.
  • Not a word-game to dodge uncomfortable texts. It is how the prophets consistently speak.
  • Not permission for vague spiritualising. Covenant language is concrete: law, temple, priesthood, access, authority.

8) New Creation Reality

If the old covenant world has been judged and removed, then you are not living under a fading administration. You live in the unshakable kingdom now: stable access, settled identity, Spirit-life, and presence. “Heaven and earth” passing is not a threat hanging over you—it’s covenant history behind you.

Practical outcome: No temple gatekeeping. No old-covenant condemnation jurisdiction. Christ’s word and priesthood define your access and your life.
