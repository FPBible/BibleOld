1 Peter

Exiles, Royal Identity, and the Fiery Trial of the Transition.

Book Summary

1 Peter is a "wartime" epistle written to the Jewish remnant (the Diaspora) as they navigated the final decade before the fall of Jerusalem. Peter prepares them for a "fiery trial"—the covenantal pressure that would consume the old world. He redefines their identity not by the physical Temple in Jerusalem, but as "living stones" in a spiritual house that no Roman legion could destroy.

Covenantal Keys for 1 Peter

  • The "At Hand" Horizon: Peter explicitly states that "the end of all things is at hand" (1 Pet 4:7), locking the fulfillment to his own generation.
  • The Living Temple: Christ is the cornerstone of a new priesthood that replaces the Levitical system, which Peter saw as nearing its end (1 Pet 2:4-9).
  • Judgment at the House of God: Peter identifies the AD 70 destruction of the Temple as the point where judgment begins with the "house of God" (the apostate Old Covenant establishment) before moving to the nations (1 Pet 4:17).
  • The Fiery Trial: Rather than a global apocalypse, this refers to the local persecution and the literal fire that would consume the Jerusalem Temple.
