Zechariah 5

The Sixth and Seventh Visions: The Curse and the Relocation of Wickedness

1. Immediate Context

Zechariah 5 presents two stark visions of judgment that follow the encouragement of the Temple-building visions in Chapters 3 and 4. The sixth vision—the Flying Scroll—is a judicial warrant representing the curses of the Law (Deuteronomy 28) entering the house of those who have violated the Covenant. The seventh vision—the Woman in the Ephah—documents the legal removal of "Wickedness" from the land of Israel. While the post-exilic remnant was focused on restoration, Zechariah reveals that the "House" (Temple) and the land must be purged of the spirit of apostasy. These visions provide the prophetic blueprint for the first-century AD 70 judgment, where the "curse" finally consumed the physical Temple and the apostate religious system was relocated to its own "place" in the land of Shinar.

2. Scripture (WEB)

1 Then I lifted up my eyes again and saw, and behold, a flying scroll. 2 He said to me, "What do you see?" I answered, "I see a flying scroll. Its length is twenty cubits, and its width ten cubits."
3 Then he said to me, "This is the curse that goes out over the surface of the whole land; for everyone who steals shall be cut off according to it on the one side; and everyone who swears falsely shall be cut off according to it on the other side. 4 I will cause it to go out," says Yahweh of Armies, "and it will enter into the house of the thief, and into the house of him who swears falsely by my name; and it will remain in the middle of his house, and will consume it with its timber and its stones."
5 Then the angel who talked with me went out and said to me, "Lift up your eyes now, and see what this is that is going out." 6 I said, "What is it?" He said, "This is the ephah basket that is going out." He said moreover, "This is their appearance in all the land— 7 (and behold, a lead cover was lifted up)—and there was a woman sitting in the middle of the ephah."
8 He said, "This is Wickedness;" and he threw her down into the middle of the ephah; and he threw the weight of lead on its mouth. 9 Then I lifted up my eyes and saw, and behold, there were two women and the wind was in their wings. Now they had wings like the wings of a stork, and they lifted up the ephah between earth and the sky.
10 Then I said to the angel who talked with me, "Where are these carrying the ephah?" 11 He said to me, "To build her a house in the land of Shinar. When it is prepared, she will be set there in her own place."

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • Dimensions of the Scroll (v. 2): 20 x 10 cubits. These are the exact dimensions of the porch of Solomon’s Temple (1 Kings 6:3). This signifies that the "curse" is legally tied to the House of God and its judicial administration.
  • Consuming Timber and Stones (v. 4): A specific prophetic detail fulfilled in AD 70. When the Roman armies set fire to the Temple, the "timber and stones" were literally consumed and dismantled, fulfilling Jesus' prophecy in Matthew 24:2.
  • The Ephah (v. 6): A standard unit of dry measure (approx. 22 liters). It symbolizes the "measure" of iniquity being filled by the apostate generation (Matthew 23:32).
  • The Lead Cover (v. 8): Represents the heavy, definitive sealing of judgment. The lead "weight" ensures that Wickedness cannot escape the decree of relocation.
  • Land of Shinar (v. 11): The site of the Tower of Babel. In apocalyptic idiom, this is "Babylon." The vision represents the removal of the harlot system from the "Holy Land" to its own place of destruction.

4. Annotations

  • Flying Scroll: (Hebrew: megillah aphah) A judicial warrant or public decree, visible to all, representing the "Curse" of the Law.
  • Curse: (Hebrew: alah) Specifically the covenantal sanctions established in Deuteronomy 28 for those who violate the Law.
  • Swears Falsely: Perjury or breaking a covenantal oath made in Yahweh’s name.
  • Wickedness: (Hebrew: risha) Personified here as a woman, representing the spiritual apostasy and corporate rebellion of the nation.
  • Wings like a Stork: Storks were "unclean" birds under the Law. Their presence indicates that the removal of wickedness is a work of divine providence using instruments of judgment.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

The Judicial Warrant against the House

In verses 1–4, the Flying Scroll establishes the legal basis for the destruction of the physical Temple. The scroll’s dimensions identify it as a "Temple decree." For centuries, the apostate leaders of Israel had "stolen" and "sworn falsely" within the sanctuary, treating the Temple as a "den of thieves." This vision declares that the Curse of the Law (Deuteronomy 28) has been unleashed. This warrant remained in "mid-air" until the first century. When Jesus stood before the Herodian Temple and declared that "not one stone will be left on another," He was announcing the execution of this specific Zechariah prophecy. In AD 70, the "timber and stones" were consumed by fire, terminating the physical administration of the Law.

The Relocation of Mystery Babylon

The vision of the Woman in the Ephah (vv. 5–11) documents the relocation of "Wickedness" from Zion to Shinar (Babylon). In the first-century transition (AD 30–70), the "faithful city" (Jerusalem) had become a "harlot" (Isaiah 1:21). John, in Revelation 17, identifies this apostate Jerusalem as "Mystery Babylon." The ephah represents the "measure" of iniquity that Jesus said the Pharisees were "filling up" (Matthew 23:32). Once the lead cover was sealed, the system was carried away to its "own place"—the Land of Shinar. This signifies that the Old Covenant religious system was legally removed from God's presence and sent to the place of confusion and judgment, allowing the true Bride (New Jerusalem) to be revealed in its stead.

8. New Creation Reality (Spiritual Instruction)

In the New Creation era, the "Flying Scroll" of the Law's curse has been legally satisfied and removed by Christ. Spiritual Instruction: You no longer live under the judicial warrant of the Law, but under the law of the Spirit of life.

The "House" of the Old Covenant was consumed because it could not contain the holiness of God. You are now the "House" built not with timber and stones, but with the indestructible life of Christ. Practical Application: Stop fearing the "curse" of the Law as if it were still flying over your life. The "Wickedness" of the old system has been relocated to Shinar; it has no "place" in the New Jerusalem where you reside. You are "sealed" not by a lead cover of judgment, but by the Holy Spirit of promise. Live in the freedom of a land that has been purged of the harlot system, recognizing that your citizenship is in the city that cannot be moved.

9. Reflection

Are you still trying to maintain "timber and stones" (rituals and physical dependencies) that the Flying Scroll was commissioned to consume, or have you moved into the spiritual reality of the New Creation?
