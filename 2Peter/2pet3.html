2 Peter 3

The Last Days, the Melting Elements, and the New Creation

The Elements (Stoicheia) Covenant Judgment AD 70
1 This is now, beloved, the second letter that I have written to you; and in both of them I stir up your sincere mind by reminding you 2 that you should remember the words which were spoken before by the holy prophets, and the commandment of us, the apostles of the Lord and Savior: 3 knowing this first, that in the last days mockers will come, walking after their own lusts, 4 and saying, “Where is the promise of his coming? For, from the day that the fathers fell asleep, all things continue as they were from the beginning of the creation.” 5 For this they willfully forget, that there were heavens from of old, and an earth formed out of water and by water, by the word of God; 6 by which means the world that then was, being overflowed with water, perished. 7 But the heavens that now are, and the earth, by the same word have been stored up for fire, being reserved against the day of judgment and destruction of ungodly men.

Fulfillment Notes

  • The Scoffers in the "Last Days": Peter is not predicting atheists in the 21st century. He is writing to first-century believers who were currently living in the biblical "last days" (the closing decades of the Old Covenant age). Jesus had promised to return in judgment before their generation passed away (Matt 24:34). As the years dragged on toward AD 70, scoffers—likely apostate Jews—began to mock the Christians, claiming the Temple and the Old Order would never fall.
  • The Flood Parallel: To refute the scoffers, Peter points to Noah's flood. When the flood came, the *physical globe* did not vanish. Rather, the *world order* of ungodly men perished.
  • Reserved for Fire: The "heavens and earth that now are" refers to the covenant world of first-century Israel (centered around the Jerusalem Temple system). Just as the "world" of Noah was judged by water while the physical planet remained, the Old Covenant "world" of Judaism was reserved for the fiery judgment of AD 70, exactly as Jesus prophesied (Matt 22:7).
8 But don’t forget this one thing, beloved, that one day is with the Lord as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day. 9 The Lord is not slow concerning his promise, as some count slowness; but is patient with us, not wishing that any should perish, but that all should come to repentance. 10 But the day of the Lord will come as a thief in the night; in which the heavens will pass away with a great noise, and the elements will be dissolved with fervent heat, and the earth and the works that are in it will be burned up.

Fulfillment Notes

  • A Thousand Years as One Day: Futurists often abuse verse 8 to claim God uses a "magic calendar" to stretch a generation into thousands of years. But Peter is directly quoting Psalm 90:4 to encourage the persecuted church. He is saying: *God has not forgotten you. The delay is not failure; it is mercy.* God was holding off the Roman armies to give the final remnant of Israel time to repent and flee the city before it was destroyed.
  • The Elements (Stoicheia): This is the key to the entire chapter. The Greek word translated "elements" is *stoicheia*. In modern English, "elements" makes us think of the periodic table (atoms, hydrogen, carbon). But the periodic table didn't exist in Peter's day. Everywhere else in the New Testament, *stoicheia* refers to the **religious laws, rituals, and elementary principles of the Old Covenant** (See Galatians 4:3, Gal 4:9, Col 2:8, Col 2:20, Heb 5:12).
  • Passing Away with a Noise: Peter is not describing nuclear fission. He is using standard prophetic idiom. The "heavens and earth" of the Old Covenant (its priesthood, its animal sacrifices, its physical temple) were about to violently dissolve in the fires of AD 70.
11 Therefore since all these things will be destroyed like this, what kind of people ought you to be in holy living and godliness, 12 looking for and earnestly desiring the coming of the day of God, which will cause the burning heavens to be dissolved, and the elements will melt with fervent heat? 13 But, according to his promise, we look for new heavens and a new earth, in which righteousness dwells. 14 Therefore, beloved, seeing that you look for these things, be diligent to be found in peace, without blemish and blameless in his sight. 15 Regard the patience of our Lord as salvation; even as our beloved brother Paul also, according to the wisdom given to him, wrote to you; 16 as also in all of his letters, speaking in them of these things. In those, there are some things that are hard to understand, which the ignorant and unsettled twist, as they also do to the other Scriptures, to their own destruction. 17 You therefore, beloved, knowing these things beforehand, beware, lest being carried away with the error of the wicked, you fall from your own steadfastness. 18 But grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. To him be the glory both now and forever. Amen.

Fulfillment Notes

  • The New Heavens and New Earth: Because the Old Covenant system (the first heaven and earth) was melting away, Peter looked forward to the full establishment of the New Covenant—the New Heavens and New Earth. This is not a floating physical paradise in the future. It is the spiritual reality we live in *right now* under Christ's eternal kingdom.
  • Paul's Hard Sayings: It is fascinating that Peter admits Paul's writings on "these things" (eschatology, the transition of the covenants, the end of the law) are hard to understand and were being twisted even back then. Today, futurism continues to twist Paul's letters by ignoring his first-century context and timeline.
  • Knowing These Things Beforehand: Peter tells his audience to beware *because the destruction was coming in their lifetime*. He urges them to remain steadfast and grow in grace as the Old World burns and the New World fully dawns.
