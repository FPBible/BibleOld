Horns: The Power of Empires

Defining the Symbol

In biblical apocalyptic literature (Daniel, Zechariah, Revelation), a "horn" represents political or military power (derived from the strength of an ox or ram). Zechariah 1:18-21 introduces four horns that scattered Judah. These typically represent the world powers—Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece, and Rome—that historically dominated Israel.

The Casting Down of the Horns

The "craftsmen" sent to terrify the horns represent God's providential deconstruction of earthly pride. Fulfilled Eschatology recognizes that the final "horn" to be dismantled in the biblical narrative was the persecuting power of the Roman-Jewish alliance in the first century. By AD 70, the horn of apostate Israel was broken, allowing the "Horn of Salvation" (Luke 1:69)—Jesus Christ—to reign supreme without a rival covenantal system.
