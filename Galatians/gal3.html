Galatians 3

Promise Fulfilled · Law Exposed · New Covenant Identity

1. Immediate Context

Paul thoroughly dismantles the theological core of law-based identity. The Galatian assemblies are being aggressively persuaded that the Torah completes what Christ began, pulling them backward into an obsolete system. Paul responds by demonstrating from redemptive history that the Old Covenant law never had the power to give life, never defined the ultimate inheritance, and was always intended to be a strictly temporary guardian until the arrival of the promised Seed, Jesus Christ.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/GAL03.htm

1 Foolish Galatians, who has bewitched you not to obey the truth, before whose eyes Jesus Christ was openly portrayed among you as crucified?
2 I just want to learn this from you: Did you receive the Spirit by the works of the law, or by hearing of faith?
3 Are you so foolish? Having begun in the Spirit, are you now completed in the flesh?
4 Did you suffer so many things in vain, if it is indeed in vain?
5 He therefore who supplies the Spirit to you and does miracles among you, does he do it by the works of the law, or by hearing of faith?
6 Even as Abraham “believed God, and it was counted to him for righteousness.”
7 Know therefore that those who are of faith are children of Abraham.
8 The Scripture, foreseeing that God would justify the Gentiles by faith, preached the Good News beforehand to Abraham, saying, “In you all the nations will be blessed.”
9 So then, those who are of faith are blessed with the faithful Abraham.
10 For as many as are of the works of the law are under a curse. For it is written, “Cursed is everyone who doesn’t continue in all things that are written in the book of the law, to do them.”
11 Now that no man is justified by the law before God is evident, for, “The righteous will live by faith.”
12 The law is not of faith, but, “The man who does them will live by them.”
13 Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law, having become a curse for us. For it is written, “Cursed is everyone who hangs on a tree,”
14 that the blessing of Abraham might come on the Gentiles through Christ Jesus, that we might receive the promise of the Spirit through faith.
15 Brothers, I speak like men. Though it is only a man’s covenant, yet when it has been confirmed, no one makes it void or adds to it.
16 Now the promises were spoken to Abraham and to his seed. He doesn’t say, “To seeds,” as of many, but as of one, “To your seed,” which is Christ.
17 Now I say this: A covenant confirmed beforehand by God in Christ, the law, which came four hundred thirty years after, does not annul, so as to make the promise of no effect.
18 For if the inheritance is of the law, it is no more of promise; but God has granted it to Abraham by promise.
19 Why then the law? It was added because of transgressions, until the seed should come to whom the promise has been made. It was ordained through angels by the hand of a mediator.
20 Now a mediator is not between one, but God is one.
21 Is the law then against the promises of God? Certainly not! For if there had been a law given which could make alive, most certainly righteousness would have been of the law.
22 But the Scripture imprisoned all things under sin, that the promise by faith in Jesus Christ might be given to those who believe.
23 But before faith came, we were kept in custody under the law, confined for the faith which should afterwards be revealed.
24 So that the law has become our tutor to bring us to Christ, that we might be justified by faith.
25 But now that faith has come, we are no longer under a tutor.
26 For you are all children of God, through faith in Christ Jesus.
27 For as many of you as were baptized into Christ have put on Christ.
28 There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free man, there is neither male nor female; for you are all one in Christ Jesus.
29 If you are Christ’s, then you are Abraham’s seed and heirs according to promise.

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • Time-Frame Statements: The phrases "until the seed should come," "before faith came," and "now that faith has come" definitively mark the strictly temporary, time-bound duration of the Old Covenant law.
  • Audience Indicators: Paul addresses the "foolish Galatians," a specific group of first-century believers who were facing immediate, real-time pressure from Judaizers to adopt Torah boundary markers.
  • Covenant Transition Markers: The entire text maps the monumental shift in redemptive history from being under the custody of a "tutor" (the fading Old Covenant) to stepping into mature sonship (the final New Covenant).

4. Annotations

  • Works of the law: Specific covenant boundary markers separating Jews from Gentiles (such as circumcision and kosher diet), not a generalized concept of good moral deeds.
  • Curse of the law: The built-in covenantal condemnation of the Torah, which demanded total compliance but offered no inherent remedy for the failure it exposed.
  • Tutor / Guardian (paidagogos): A temporary custodian assigned to supervise a minor in ancient society. The Old Covenant functioned as a strict disciplinarian until the maturity of the New Creation arrived.
  • Seed: A corporate identity rooted strictly in union with Christ, representing the true, spiritual fulfillment of the promise rather than mere biological descent from Abraham.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

The Spirit Validates the Covenant Shift (v. 1-5)

Paul begins not with abstract theory, but with the Galatians' undeniable, lived experience. He challenges them to remember how they received the Holy Spirit: it was through hearing with faith, not through adherence to Torah regulations. The outpouring of the Spirit was the universally recognized prophetic marker of the promised New Covenant (Ezekiel 36, Joel 2).

If God bypassed the law entirely to grant them the ultimate seal of the New Creation, then to turn back to the law for completion is sheer folly. It is an attempt to finish in the flesh what was birthed by the Spirit. In the landscape of fulfilled prophecy, this confirms that the covenant transition was already functionally underway; New Covenant life cannot be sustained by Old Covenant mechanics.

Abraham Predates and Defines the Promise (v. 6-9)

To dismantle the Judaizers' argument, Paul reaches far behind Moses directly to Abraham. This is a brilliant and decisive covenantal maneuver. The promise that God would justify the nations by faith was established centuries before the giving of the law at Sinai. Therefore, the law cannot redefine the terms of inheritance.

God's covenant with Abraham was based entirely on promise and trust, not on a legal code of performance. By showing that the true children of Abraham are those who share his faith, Paul completely removes biological descent and Torah adherence as the prerequisites for entering the family of God.

The Curse and Its Redemptive Reversal (v. 10-14)

Paul does not treat the "curse of the law" as a mystical, generalized doom; it is the specific, legal consequence built into the Sinai covenant itself. The law demanded absolute, comprehensive obedience, and inherently condemned anyone who failed. Because the law provided no ultimate cure, anyone relying on it for justification lived constantly under its shadow of condemnation.

Christ's crucifixion was the definitive covenantal transaction. He did not absorb random punishment; He legally absorbed the specific curse of the broken Old Covenant system, effectively ending its jurisdiction. By taking the verdict of the law upon Himself, He opened the way for the blessing of Abraham—the promised Spirit—to flood out to the nations without the barrier of the Torah.

The Strictly Temporary Nature of the Law (v. 15-22)

One of the most critical elements of fulfilled eschatology is recognizing the temporary nature of the Mosaic economy. Paul explicitly states the law was "added because of transgressions, until the seed should come." The law was never Plan B, nor was it intended to be eternal. It was a parenthesis in redemptive history.

Its purpose was to expose and restrain sin, acting as a holding cell ("imprisoned under sin") until the arrival of the promised Deliverer. The law could diagnose the disease of the human condition, but it could never impart life. It was a temporary administration entirely subordinate to the overarching, eternal promise given to Abraham.

From Captives Under a Guardian to Full Heirs (v. 23-29)

Paul uses the cultural image of a paidagogos—a strict disciplinarian assigned to a child—to describe the Old Covenant. "Before faith came" does not mean before individual people learned how to believe; it refers to the historical epoch prior to the arrival of Christ. For centuries, God's people were kept in custody under the rigorous boundaries of the law.

But "now that faith has come," the era of the guardian is decisively and permanently over. Through baptism into Christ, believers put on a completely new identity. The profound realities of the New Creation obliterate the old dividing lines of the flesh—Jew or Greek, slave or free, male or female. Status is no longer determined by the old world's markers. If you belong to Christ, you are the true seed, standing fully justified as an heir to the promise.

8. New Creation Reality

Galatians 3 permanently ends the exhausting cycle of spiritual comparison and performance. You are not waiting to qualify for God's love, nor are you progressing up a ladder toward belonging. If you are in Christ, you are already a full heir, completely free from the guardian of the old law. Any religious system that reintroduces hierarchy, boundary markers, or delay inherently denies the promise and attempts to revive a guardian that Christ has already dismissed.

9. Reflection

Where might I still be acting as though my inheritance must be earned through performance, rather than resting in the finished work of Christ?
