1. Immediate Context

Revelation 11 concludes the powerful interlude that began in Chapter 10. Before the final, seventh trumpet sounds to declare the absolute transfer of the Kingdom, John is given a measuring rod. He is commanded to measure the inner sanctuary of the temple but abandon the outer courts to the Gentile nations.

This chapter introduces the "Two Witnesses" whose prophetic testimony plagues the apostate "Great City." More importantly, verse 8 gives us the undeniable geographical anchor for the entire book: The Great City that is about to be destroyed is the exact place where Jesus was crucified. It is first-century Jerusalem.

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • The Identification of the Great City (v. 8): This verse is the interpretive key to Revelation. The "great city" is spiritually called Sodom and Egypt, but geographically it is explicitly "where also their Lord was crucified." Jesus was crucified outside Jerusalem. The Harlot City destined for destruction is apostate, first-century Jerusalem, not a revived future Babylon or the city of Rome.
  • Forty-Two Months (v. 2): This translates to 3.5 years (or 1,260 days). This is the exact historical length of the Jewish-Roman war. In the Spring of AD 67, the Roman Emperor Nero formally dispatched Vespasian to crush the Jewish rebellion. Exactly three and a half years later, in the late Summer of AD 70, the temple was completely destroyed and the "outer court" was thoroughly trampled by the Gentile armies.
  • Not Rebuilding a Physical Temple: Futurists claim verse 1 proves a third physical temple must be built in modern Jerusalem. But John is told to measure the worshipers inside it. The inner sanctuary represents the true, spiritual New Covenant Church, safely preserved in Christ while the outer, physical, obsolete Jewish system is given over to destruction.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

1) Measuring the True Temple (vv. 1‚Äì2)

John is handed a measuring rod and told to measure the temple, the altar, and the people worshiping inside, but to abandon the outer court to the Gentiles. For decades, the Apostles taught that the physical stone building in Jerusalem was obsolete and that the believers were now the true Temple of the Holy Spirit (1 Corinthians 3:16, Ephesians 2:21).

God is visually declaring a covenantal boundary. He is claiming ownership and offering spiritual protection over the true, inner sanctuary (the New Covenant Church). The physical, external trappings of the Old Covenant religion (the outer courts and the city of Jerusalem itself) are completely abandoned to the Roman armies to be trampled underfoot for 42 literal months (AD 67 to AD 70).

2) The Two Witnesses (vv. 3‚Äì6)

Futurists spend endless energy trying to figure out which two literal men will return to the modern Middle East to breathe literal fire. But John is using prophetic idiom. Under biblical law, it required the testimony of two witnesses to legally condemn someone to death (Deuteronomy 17:6).

These witnesses represent the faithful, testifying Apostolic Church. They are imbued with the authority of Moses (water to blood) and Elijah (shutting the sky). They stand in the streets of Jerusalem proclaiming the New Covenant Gospel and validating the impending covenant lawsuit against the nation. The "fire from their mouth" is the consuming power of the prophetic Word of God (Jeremiah 5:14).

3) The Murder in the Great City (vv. 7‚Äì10)

When their testimony is finished, the Beast from the abyss (the Roman persecuting power, incited by the Jewish leaders) makes war against the Church. Their dead bodies are left in the streets of the "great city."

Verse 8 is the devastating blow to all modern futurism. It permanently identifies the "Great City" of Revelation: "Where also their Lord was crucified." Jesus was not crucified in Rome. He was not crucified in New York or modern Iraq. He was crucified in Jerusalem. Apostate Jerusalem had become spiritually identical to Sodom (morally depraved) and Egypt (an oppressor holding God's true people captive). When the early Christian martyrs were slaughtered, the apostate leaders of the land rejoiced.

4) Vindication and the Earthquake (vv. 11‚Äì14)

The rejoicing of the wicked is short-lived. After three and a half days (symbolic of a short, restricted period, mirroring Christ's time in the grave), the breath of life enters the witnesses. They are vindicated publicly.

"Come up here!" is the ultimate validation of their testimony. While the faithful Church is vindicated, a massive "earthquake" (covenantal shaking) strikes the city. A tenth of the city falls. The remnant of Israel that survives this localized shaking is terrified and finally gives glory to the God of heaven.

5) The Seventh Trumpet: The Kingdom Transfer (vv. 15‚Äì19)

The final trumpet sounds. This does not result in the explosion of planet Earth. It results in a legal declaration: "The kingdom of the world has become the Kingdom of our Lord." The covenant transition is complete. The Old Order is legally terminated, and Christ's New Covenant reign is universally established.

Notice verse 19: The temple of God in heaven is opened, and the Ark of the Covenant is seen. Under the Old Covenant, the Ark was hidden behind a thick veil, completely inaccessible to the people. But now, because the earthly shadow is being destroyed, the true heavenly reality is thrown wide open. Complete, unhindered access to the presence of God is permanently granted to the Church.
