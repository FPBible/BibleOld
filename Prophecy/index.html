Prophecy Hub

Fulfilled Judgment · Covenant Transition · New Creation

How to Read This Hub

Biblical prophecy does not describe the end of the physical universe. It announces covenant judgment, the removal of the Old Covenant order, and the establishment of the New Covenant world in Christ.

These pages exist to clarify prophetic idioms and New Testament fulfillment without futurist timelines, speculative charts, or fear-based readings. Scripture interprets Scripture. The New Testament governs the meaning.

I. Covenant Judgment Texts

These texts announce the transition of ages: judgment on the Old Covenant system, vindication of Christ, and the public end of the temple-centered world. They are audience-relevant and fulfilled within the first century.

  • Matthew 24 — Judgment on Jerusalem (Olivet Discourse)
  • Luke 21 — Days of Vengeance and Vindication
  • Daniel — Kingdom, Son of Man, and Time
  • Isaiah — New Heavens, New Earth, New Humanity
  • Ezekiel 37 — Resurrection as Covenant Restoration
  • Psalms — Enthronement and Rule
  • 2 Peter 3 — Heavens and Earth as Covenant Transition
  • Revelation — Symbol, Judgment, Victory

II. Presence and Parousia Clarified

These texts explain Christ’s “coming” as royal presence and covenant vindication — not escape theology, not delay theology, and not end-of-planet language.

  • 1 Thessalonians 4 — Presence, Not Escape

III. Resurrection Explained (Interpretive Framework)

These writings are not “prophecy announcements.” They define resurrection in light of Christ: what it means, how it functions, and how it relates to covenant life and victory.

  • 1 Corinthians 15 — Resurrection and Victory

IV. Core Covenant Themes (Support Pages)

These pages keep chapter commentary clean by centralising repeat themes. Use them whenever a chapter leans heavily on covenant identity language.

  • New Creation — Fulfilled Covenant Reality
  • New Covenant — Internalized Law, Direct Access, New Creation Life
  • Temple — Judgment on the Old Center, Replacement in Christ
  • Wilderness — Exodus Pattern, Testing, and Transition

V. Judgment Terms Explained (Commonly Misread)

These pages clarify high-confusion terms that are routinely flattened into modern assumptions. They are interpretive control pages: link to them instead of re-litigating the topic in every chapter.

  • Hell — Judgment Language in Its Biblical Context
  • Satan — The Adversary as Accuser and Covenant Opponent

Important Note

Prophetic symbolism must be interpreted by Scripture itself. Where the New Testament explains resurrection, judgment, kingdom, “heavens and earth,” or “new creation,” those explanations govern how prophecy is read.

How to Read This Hub

The Bible’s prophetic language does not describe the end of the physical universe. It announces covenant judgment, the removal of the Old Covenant order, and the establishment of the New Covenant world in Christ. These pages explain how Scripture itself uses prophetic imagery and how the New Testament declares its fulfilment.

These texts announce the covenant transition using symbolic and apocalyptic language drawn from Israel’s Scriptures. They are time-bound, audience-relevant, and fulfilled within the first century.

  • Matthew 24 — Judgment on Jerusalem
  • Luke 21 — Days of Vengeance
  • Daniel — Kingdom, Son of Man, and Time
  • Isaiah — New Heavens, New Earth, New Humanity
  • Ezekiel 37 — Resurrection as Restoration
  • Psalms — Enthronement and Rule
  • 2 Peter 3 — Heavens and Earth
  • Revelation — Symbol, Judgment, Victory

These texts explain how Christ’s “coming” is described in Scripture — not as physical escape or delay, but as royal presence, covenant vindication, and historical judgment on the old order.

These writings are not “prophetic announcements.” They define and interpret resurrection in the light of Christ’s finished work and the completed covenant transition.
