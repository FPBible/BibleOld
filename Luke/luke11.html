Luke 11

The Sovereign Prayer • Binding the Strong Man • The Woe of the Experts

1. Immediate Context

In Luke 11, the glory of the Son is revealed through the direct communication of the New Creation. During the AD 30–70 transition, the disciples requested to be taught how to pray, prompting the Messiah to provide a blueprint that bypasses the repetitive, ritualistic "performance" of the old world. This prayer is a jurisdictional claim: it asks for the Father's Kingdom to be manifested and His will to be established, moving the focus from earthly Temple rituals to the direct presence of the Father.

The chapter reaches a legal turning point as the Son is accused of operating by the power of Beelzebul. His response—the binding of the "Strong Man"—is a definitive legal enactment of the transition. He proves that the New Creation is a superior force that has come to plunder the house of the old age, reclaiming humanity from demonic and ritualistic bondage. The chapter culminates in a blistering indictment of the "experts" in the Law. These professional mediators are charged with "taking away the key of knowledge," essentially locking the people out of the very Kingdom that had arrived. As the AD 70 horizon loomed, this chapter served as the final legal warning to the institutional guardians that their time of occupation was over.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/LUK11.htm

1 It happened, that when he was praying in a certain place, when he ceased, one of his disciples said to him, “Lord, teach us to pray, even as John also taught his disciples.”
2 He said to them, “When you pray, say, ‘Our Father in heaven, may your name be kept holy. May your Kingdom come. May your will be done on earth, as it is in heaven. 3 Give us day by day our daily bread. 4 Forgive us our sins, for we ourselves also forgive everyone who is indebted to us. Bring us not into temptation, but deliver us from the evil one.’”
5 He said to them, “Which of you, if you go to a friend at midnight, and tell him, ‘Friend, lend me three loaves of bread, 6 for a friend of mine has come to me from a journey, and I have nothing to set before him,’ 7 and he from within will answer and say, ‘Don’t trouble me. The door is now shut, and my children are with me in bed. I can’t rise and give it to you’? 8 I tell you, although he will not rise and give it to him because he is his friend, yet because of his persistence he will get up and give him as many as he needs.
9 “I tell you, keep asking, and it will be given you. Keep seeking, and you will find. Keep knocking, and it will be opened to you. 10 For everyone who asks receives. He who seeks finds. To him who knocks it will be opened. 11 “Which of you fathers, if your son asks for bread, will give him a stone? Or if he asks for a fish, he won’t give him a snake instead of a fish, will he? 12 Or if he asks for an egg, he won’t give him a scorpion, will he? 13 If you then, being evil, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your heavenly Father give the Holy Spirit to those who ask him?”
14 He was casting out a demon, and it was mute. It happened, when the demon had gone out, the mute man spoke; and the multitudes marveled. 15 But some of them said, “He casts out demons by Beelzebul, the prince of the demons.” 16 Others, testing him, sought from him a sign from heaven. 17 But he, knowing their thoughts, said to them, “Every kingdom divided against itself is brought to desolation. A house divided against a house falls. 18 If Satan also is divided against himself, how will his kingdom stand? For you say that I cast out demons by Beelzebul. 19 But if I by Beelzebul cast out demons, by whom do your children cast them out? Therefore they will be your judges. 20 But if I by the finger of God cast out demons, then God’s Kingdom has come to you.
21 “When the strong man, fully armed, guards his own dwelling, his goods are safe. 22 But when someone stronger attacks him and overcomes him, he takes from him his whole armor in which he trusted, and divides his spoils.
23 “He who is not with me is against me. He who doesn’t gather with me scatters. 24 The unclean spirit, when he has gone out of the man, passes through waterless places, seeking rest, and finding none, it says, ‘I will turn back to my house from which I came out.’ 25 When it comes, it finds it swept and put in order. 26 Then it goes, and takes seven other spirits more evil than itself, and they enter in and dwell there. The last state of that man becomes worse than the first.”
27 It happened, as he said these things, that a certain woman out of the multitude lifted up her voice, and said to him, “Blessed is the womb that bore you, and the breasts which nursed you!”
28 But he said, “On the contrary, blessed are those who hear the word of God, and keep it.”
29 When the multitudes were gathering together, he began to say, “This is an evil generation. It seeks after a sign. No sign will be given to it but the sign of Jonah the prophet. 30 For even as Jonah became a sign to the Ninevites, so will also the Son of Man be to this generation. 31 The Queen of the South will rise up in the judgment with the men of this generation, and will condemn them: for she came from the ends of the earth to hear the wisdom of Solomon; and behold, someone greater than Solomon is here. 32 The men of Nineveh will stand up in the judgment with this generation, and will condemn it: for they repented at the preaching of Jonah; and behold, someone greater than Jonah is here.
33 “No one, when he has lit a lamp, puts it in a cellar or under a basket, but on a stand, that those who come in may see the light. 34 The lamp of the body is the eye. Therefore when your eye is good, your whole body is also full of light; but when it is evil, your body also is full of darkness. 35 Therefore see whether the light that is in you isn’t darkness. 36 If therefore your whole body is full of light, having no part dark, it will be wholly full of light, as when the lamp with its bright shining gives you light.”
37 Now as he spoke, a certain Pharisee asked him to dine with him. He went in, and sat at the table. 38 When the Pharisee saw it, he marveled that he had not first washed himself before dinner. 39 The Lord said to him, “Now you Pharisees cleanse the outside of the cup and of the platter, but your inward part is full of extortion and wickedness. 40 You foolish ones, didn’t he who made the outside make the inside also? 41 But give for gifts to the needy those things which are within, and behold, all things will be clean to you. 42 “But woe to you Pharisees! For you tithe mint and rue and every herb, but you bypass justice and the love of God. You ought to have done these, and not to have left the other undone. 43 Woe to you Pharisees! For you love the best seats in the synagogues, and the greetings in the marketplaces. 44 Woe to you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! For you are like hidden graves, and the men who walk over them don’t know it.”
45 One of the lawyers answered him, “Teacher, in saying this you insult us also.” 46 He said, “Woe to you lawyers also! For you load men with burdens that are difficult to carry, and you yourselves won’t even lift one finger to help carry those burdens. 47 Woe to you! For you build the tombs of the prophets, and your fathers killed them. 48 So you testify and consent to the works of your fathers. For they killed them, and you build their tombs. 49 Therefore also the wisdom of God said, ‘I will send to them prophets and apostles; and some of them they will kill and persecute, 50 that the blood of all the prophets, which was shed from the foundation of the world, may be required of this generation; 51 from the blood of Abel to the blood of Zechariah, who perished between the altar and the sanctuary.’ Yes, I tell you, it will be required of this generation. 52 Woe to you lawyers! For you took away the key of knowledge. You didn’t enter in yourselves, and those who were entering in, you hindered.” 53 As he said these things to them, the scribes and the Pharisees began to be terribly angry, and to draw many things out of him; 54 lying in wait for him, and seeking to catch him in something he might say, that they might accuse him.

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • God’s Kingdom (v. 2, 20): The present reality of the transition. The Son declares that if He casts out demons by the "finger of God," then the Kingdom has legally arrived in their midst.
  • This Generation (v. 29, 31, 32, 50, 51): The specific AD 30–70 audience. The Son repeatedly anchors the judgment, the sign, and the accountability for prophetic blood to the contemporary first-century generation.
  • Sign of Jonah (v. 30): A definitive prophecy of the Son's death and resurrection, which would serve as the final legal sign to the old world before its dissolution.
  • The Key of Knowledge (v. 52): A legal indictment of the religious professionals. They possessed the scriptures but used them to lock the doors of the New Creation rather than open them.

4. Annotations

  • The Finger of God (v. 20) — A reference to the power that wrote the Law on stone tablets (Exodus 31:18). Now, that same power is dismantling the demonic order to write the New Covenant on hearts.
  • The Strong Man (v. 21) — A symbol of the adversary’s legal hold on humanity through the curse and the Law. The Son is the "Stronger One" who evicts him.
  • Hidden Graves (v. 44) — A ritual idiom. Walking over a grave made a person "unclean." Jesus is saying that the religious leaders are sources of defilement that appear as "life" on the surface.
  • Key of Knowledge (v. 52) — Represents the authority to interpret and grant access to the meaning of the Kingdom. The "Thieves" of the old world stole this key to preserve their own status.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

1) The Sovereign Communication of the New World

The teaching on prayer (Luke 11:2-4) is the legal constitution of New Creation fellowship. The disciples, weary of the "Exhausting Performance" of the Pharisaic ritual, recognized that the Son possessed a direct line to the Father. The blueprint He provides—"Our Father"—is a declaration of adoption and direct access. It asks for the Kingdom to be manifested on earth as a present jurisdictional reality. This prayer moves the believer from the role of a religious debtor to a child receiving "daily bread." The glory of the Son is unveiled here as the Mediator who brings the Father's heart into the street. By the end of the transition (AD 70), this prayer was no longer a request for a future hope, but the established atmosphere of the Royal Priesthood, living in the light of a Kingdom that had fully come.

2) Binding the Strong Man: A Legal Eviction

The "Strong Man" parable (Luke 11:21-22) is the definitive legal summary of the Messiah's mission. The adversary—the "Strong Man"—had held humanity in legal custody through the curse. The Son’s arrival as the "Stronger One" was not a negotiation but an attack. By casting out demons, He was physically demonstrating that He had already bound the adversary and was now "plundering" his house—reclaiming the souls and the Land. This manifestation of glory proved that the New Creation had superior jurisdictional force. As the AD 70 horizon approached, this plundering reached its climax, resulting in the total eviction of the demonic and ritualistic powers of the old age, leaving the Son as the sole Sovereign over the New World.

3) The Sign of Jonah and the Finality of Judgment

The Son’s refusal to give a "sign from heaven" (Luke 11:29-32) was a legal verdict against the first-century generation. They were surrounded by the manifest majesty of God but demanded a performance-based validation. The "Sign of Jonah"—the death and resurrection of the Messiah—was the final legal notice. Just as Nineveh repented at Jonah's preaching, the "Queen of the South" and the men of Nineveh would stand in the judgment of AD 70 to condemn that generation. The glory of the Son is "someone greater than Solomon" and "someone greater than Jonah." This commentary affirms that the transition was the period of final testimony; those who refused the Light were destined for the darkness of the cellar, while the New Creation community became the lamp on the stand.

4) The Foreclosure of the Religious Professionals

The woes against the Pharisees and lawyers (Luke 11:39-52) are the formal articles of foreclosure on the old religious administration. The Son indicts them for cleaning the "outside of the cup" while their inward parts were full of extortion. By tithing mint and rue but bypassing justice, they were performing a hollow religion that had lost its legal standing. The most severe charge was "taking away the key of knowledge"—using the scriptures to prevent the people from entering the New Creation. This "Thievery" of spiritual access was the reason that the blood of all the prophets, from Abel to Zechariah, was "required of this generation." In AD 70, the physical Temple and the professional class were removed, finalizing the transition to a Royal Priesthood where every believer holds the key through direct union with the King.

8. New Creation Reality

In Luke 11, the glory of the Son is revealed as the power that binds your "Strong Man" and grants you the "Key of Knowledge." As a citizen of the New Creation, you are the house that has been plundered of its darkness and filled with Light. You do not live in the "Woe" of religious performance, trying to clean the outside of a ritual cup. You are the "Child" of the Father, with direct access to the Holy Spirit and a "Daily Bread" that is provided by the King's decree. The "Thief" who tried to lock the door of knowledge has been legally evicted. You possess the "Sign of Jonah"—the resurrected life that has already passed through judgment. You are invited to "keep asking" and "keep seeking," knowing that the Father’s response is already established in the Finished Work of the Son.

9. Reflection

Question: If the Son declared that "all things have been delivered" to Him (Luke 10:22) and that He has already bound the "Strong Man," why do we still act as though we need titled "mediators" or modern "experts" to grant us access to the Father or His Word?
