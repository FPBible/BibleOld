John

Life in the Son · New Creation Revealed

How to Read John
John’s Gospel is written to reveal life that is already present in Christ. Eternal life, resurrection, judgment, and “the hour” are not postponed realities, but covenant realities unveiled through Jesus’ death, resurrection, and reign. John must be read through the completed work of Christ and the fulfilled transition from the old covenant world into the New Creation.

John presents Jesus not merely as a teacher or miracle-worker, but as the embodiment of God’s life entering a death-bound covenant system. Signs, discourses, and confrontations are structured to show the replacement of the old order with living reality in the Son.

Key Themes in John
• Eternal life as present participation in God’s life
• Resurrection as life breaking into covenant death
• Judgment as light confronting darkness now
• “The hour” as the climax of covenant transition
• Jesus as the true Temple, Bread, Shepherd, and Vine
