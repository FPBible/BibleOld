Is the "Second Coming" a Biblical Phrase?

Examining the gap between modern tradition and the first-century covenantal vocabulary.

Linguistic Analysis Hebrews 9:28 Covenant Transition

1. The Silence of Scripture & The Trap of the Label

If you were to open a concordance and search for the specific term **"Second Coming,"** you would find that the Holy Spirit never saw fit to use it. What is in the Bible is the promise that He would "appear a second time" (Hebrews 9:28). What is not in the Bible is the capitalized, theological title "The Second Coming".

By naming the event the "Second Coming," we have inadvertently framed Christ as a King who went away on a long vacation and will eventually return in a biological body. This creates a theology of **absence**. However, the New Testament writers were not concerned with a return; they were concerned with a manifestation.

2. Understanding the Greek Vocabulary

The Apostles used three specific Greek words to describe the climax of the age, none of which carry the biological or chronological baggage of "coming back".

A) Parousia (παρουσία) — Presence

Used 24 times, this word literally means "being alongside." It describes a state of presence. In the first century, it specifically referred to the "royal visitation" of a King to a city. When an Emperor made a parousia, it was the arrival of his authority to judge, reward, and reorder the government of that city.

B) Apocalypsis (ἀποκάλυψις) — Unveiling

This describes the pulling back of a curtain. It suggests that Christ was already there, but hidden from sight behind the "veil" of the Old Covenant system. The apocalypsis was the public revealing of the fact that Jesus was already enthroned.

C) Epiphaneia (ἐπιφάνεια) — Manifestation

This word denotes a "shining forth." It is the moment a light is turned on in a dark room. The room didn't change; the light simply made the reality visible.

3. The High Priest Logic (Hebrews 9:28)

Futurists often point to Hebrews 9:28 as their "smoking gun" because it is the only verse in the Bible that uses the word "second".

"...so Christ also, having been offered once to bear the sins of many, will appear a second time, without sin, to those who are eagerly waiting for him for salvation."

To a modern Westerner, this looks like a chronological timeline. To a first-century Jew, this was **Day of Atonement code**. On the Day of Atonement, the High Priest would:

  • First Appearance: Appear before the people to take the sacrifice.
  • The Absence: Disappear behind the veil into the Holy of Holies to present the blood.
  • Second Appearance: Emerge from behind the veil to show the people the sacrifice was accepted.

Hebrews 9 is arguing that Christ's "second appearing" was His emergence from the "heavenly sanctuary" to officially end the Old Covenant age. This was the **liturgical completion** of the Atonement that happened in AD 70.

Historical Reality: The End of the Sanctuary

Historical Proof: This relief from the Arch of Titus depicts the Menorah and other Temple vessels being carried away as spoils of war in AD 70. This is the visual confirmation of Hebrews 9:8—"the way into the Holy Place wasn’t yet revealed while the first tabernacle was still standing." When the physical sanctuary fell, the High Priest's "second appearance" was finalized in history.

4. Not a Physical Descent: From Shadow to Substance

A major hurdle in understanding the "appearing" of Christ is the modern insistence on a physical, biological descent. But this demands that the New Covenant operate by Old Covenant rules.

The Language of "Cloud Comings"

In the Old Testament, "coming on the clouds" was recognized prophetic idiom for divine judgment and presence. When Jesus told the High Priest He would be seen "coming on the clouds of heaven" (Matthew 26:64), He was declaring the end of the first-century religious establishment.

A Spiritual Kingdom Cannot Have a Physical Climax

Jesus was incredibly clear about the nature of His Kingdom: "The kingdom of God does not come with observation... For indeed, the kingdom of God is in your midst" (Luke 17:20-21).

The Greek phrase entos hymon is corporate—it refers to the Kingdom being present in the Person and ministry of Christ among the people. If the Kingdom is a corporate reality present 'in the midst' of that generation, then the climax of that Kingdom—the Parousia—must also be spiritual and covenantal, not a return to localized physical limitations.

5. The Vindication of the Martyrs

The "coming" of Christ was also the legal resolution of a 40-year court case. Jesus promised that all the righteous blood shed on the earth would be required of "this generation" (Matt 23:35-36). The Parousia was the moment the Judge entered the courtroom, vindicated His people, and passed sentence on the persecutors in AD 70.

6. Why It Matters Today

If we are still waiting for a "Second Coming," we are essentially saying that the Atonement process is still unfinished—that our High Priest is still "stuck" in the sanctuary.

But the fulfilled view recognizes that the High Priest has emerged. The Parousia has been manifested. The Old World is gone, and we now live in the **unshakable Kingdom** (Hebrews 12:28).

The Shift: Tradition says "He is coming." Scripture says "He is here." New Creation life begins when you stop looking at the horizon for a rescue and start looking at the Throne for your current reality.
