New Covenant

Internalized Law, Direct Access, Indwelling Spirit

The New Covenant is not “new religion.” It is covenant replacement: from external regulation to internal life, from priestly mediation to direct access, from temple-centered identity to Spirit-indwelt union.

Key Themes

  • Law written on hearts: internal transformation, not external control.
  • Knowing God: access is no longer gated by temple systems.
  • Forgiveness and cleansing: not repeated sacrifice, but completed work.
  • New Creation living: life from union, not performance.

Where it lands in John

  • John 2 uses “new wine” and “new temple” signals — not as decoration, but as covenant transition language.
  • John 3–7 develops new birth, living water, Spirit, and worship beyond location.
