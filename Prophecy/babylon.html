Babylon / Harlot / City

How Revelation Names a Covenant Crisis

This page is a framework stub: it exists so chapter pages can link here instead of improvising definitions.

1) What this symbol is doing

“Babylon” in Revelation functions as a prophetic label: it gathers covenant unfaithfulness, persecution, and judgement imagery into one symbolic “city” identity.

2) What this is NOT

  • Not a modern nation-state prediction chart.
  • Not a future end-of-world countdown device.
  • Not a license for speculative newspaper exegesis.

3) How to read the city language

Revelation uses OT prophetic patterns (cities as covenant identities, harlotry as unfaithfulness, cup/wrath as judgement vocabulary). The interpretive control is audience relevance and first-century horizon, not modern geopolitics.
