Romans 6

Dead to the Old World, Alive in the New

Scripture: WEB Fulfillment Notes Prophecy Commentary

Original text: Romans 6 — WEB

Immediate Context

Romans 6 answers a misunderstanding created by grace. If righteousness is given apart from the Law, does that mean sin no longer matters?

Paul’s answer is not moral panic. It is identity. Believers no longer belong to the covenant world where sin and death ruled.

Note: Scripture text is from the World English Bible (WEB). See the link above for the full public-domain source.

Fulfillment Notes

  1. “Died to sin” is covenant identity language: you have changed realms and allegiance in Christ.
  2. Baptism/immersion marks transfer from the old covenant world into New Creation life, not a repeatable “reset.”
  3. Sin’s reign is tied to Law’s jurisdiction; grace means a new authority, not “permission.”
  4. “Death” and “life” are covenant outcomes: the Adamic/old-order rule ends; Christ’s life governs now.
  5. This is present-tense: Paul declares sin’s rule ended for believers, not postponed to the distant future.

Prophecy Commentary

1) Romans 6 is about identity, not effort

Paul does not command believers to die to sin. He declares that they already have. The point is not “try harder,” but “live from what is true in Christ.”

2) Baptism marks transition, not repetition

Immersion signifies passage from one covenant world to another—out of Adamic/old-order rule into Christ’s life. The emphasis is transfer of belonging, not a cycle of re-entry.

3) Sin loses authority when Law ends

In Paul’s logic, sin’s power is connected to Law’s jurisdiction. With the Law fulfilled and removed as covenant administration, sin no longer reigns as a master over those in Christ.

4) Obedience flows from belonging

Righteous living emerges because identity has changed, not to make the change happen. Grace doesn’t leave you where you were; it re-homes you.

Prophecy Hub Connections

  • Death defeated covenantally — 1 Corinthians 15
  • End of the old covenant world — 2 Peter 3

New Creation Reality

Freedom is not the absence of struggle. Freedom is knowing which world you belong to.

Believers do not fight sin to become alive. They live alive because sin’s authority has already ended for those under grace.

Reflection

Where might I still think of myself as someone trying to die, instead of someone who already lives?
