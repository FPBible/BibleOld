2 Peter 2

The Swift Destruction of False Teachers

Context: Judgment at Hand Status: Rescue of the Godly

Scripture (WEB)

1But there also arose false prophets among the people, as among you also there will be false teachers, who will secretly bring in destructive heresies, denying even the Master who bought them, bringing on themselves swift destruction. 3In covetousness they will exploit you with deceptive words: whose sentence now from of old doesn’t linger, and their destruction doesn’t slumber. 4For if God didn’t spare angels when they sinned, but cast them down to Tartarus, and committed them to pits of darkness, to be reserved for judgment; 5and didn’t spare the ancient world, but preserved Noah with seven others, a preacher of righteousness, when he brought a flood on the world of the ungodly; 6and turning the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah into ashes, condemned them to destruction, having made them an example to those who would live ungodly; 9the Lord knows how to deliver the godly out of temptation, and to keep the unrighteous under punishment for the day of judgment;

Fulfillment Commentary

Swift Destruction (v. 1-3)

Peter warns his readers of false teachers who were about to secretly bring in heresies. He emphasizes that their "destruction doesn't slumber" and their sentence "doesn't linger". If this destruction was thousands of years away, Peter's language of imminence would be deceptive. From a fulfilled perspective, these teachers were the internal rot of the first-century church that faced judgment in the AD 70 collapse of the Judean order.

The Prototype of Judgment (v. 4-6)

Peter uses three historical examples of judgment—fallen angels, the Flood, and Sodom—to set a precedent for what was about to happen in his generation. He calls these "examples" (Greek: hypodeigma) for those who would live ungodly. Just as God brought an end to those previous "worlds" or covenantal eras, He was about to bring an end to the Old Covenant world.

Rescue and Reservation (v. 9)

Peter assures the "godly" that the Lord knows how to "deliver" them. This mirrored Christ's warning in the Olivet Discourse for the faithful to flee to the mountains when they saw the Roman armies approaching Jerusalem. While the unrighteous were being "kept under punishment" for the day of judgment (the siege and fall of the city), the faithful remnant was preserved.
