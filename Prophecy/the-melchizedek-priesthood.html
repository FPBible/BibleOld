The Order of Melchizedek

The Forbidden Union

Under the Law of Moses, the offices of King and Priest were strictly separated. This division represented the inherent limitation of the Old Covenant. When Zechariah was commanded to place a crown on the head of Joshua the High Priest (Zechariah 6:11), it was a prophetic "shattering" of that legal boundary. It signaled the arrival of a new order where the altar and the throne would finally meet.

A Better Hope

The author of Hebrews (Chapter 7) argues that if the Levitical priesthood were sufficient, there would be no need for a priest like Melchizedek. This ancient figure, who met Abraham, held both titles simultaneously. In the Covenant Transition of the first century, Jesus Christ was revealed as the true Melchizedek. He did not inherit His office through genealogy, but through "the power of an indestructible life."

The Counsel of Peace

Zechariah 6:13 states that the "counsel of peace" shall be between the kingly and priestly offices. In Christ, mercy and justice are no longer at odds. He rules with authority but mediates with perfect sympathy. This is the present reality of the New Creation: we serve a King who is also our High Priest, providing an unshakeable peace that the Old Covenant could never offer.
