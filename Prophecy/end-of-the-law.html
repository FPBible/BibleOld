The End of the Law

Understanding the legal termination of the Old Covenant in AD 70.

Covenantal Fulfillment Hebrews 10:9 AD 70

1. The Misunderstood "Jot and Tittle"

The most common argument against the "End of the Law" is a misreading of Matthew 5:17-18.

"Don’t think that I came to destroy the law or the prophets. I didn’t come to destroy, but to fulfill. For most certainly I tell you, until heaven and earth pass away, not even one smallest letter or one tiny pen stroke shall in any way pass away from the law, **until all things are accomplished**." (WEB)

Notice the condition: the Law remains until all is accomplished. If any part of the Law (temple, priesthood, sacrifice) is still binding today, it means Christ failed to "accomplish all." But if all was accomplished in His finished work and the destruction of the Old World in AD 70, then the Law has legally passed away.

2. The Legal Mechanism: Removal to Establish

The New Testament does not describe a "patch" or an "update" to the Law. It describes a total replacement.

"He takes away the first, that he may establish the second."
— Hebrews 10:9

The High Priestly logic of Hebrews is clear: the two covenants cannot legally occupy the same space. The "first" (the Law) had to be taken away to make room for the "second" (the New Covenant). As long as the physical Temple stood and the Levitical priests served, the "first" was still "having standing." The fall of the Temple in AD 70 was the final, historical eviction of the "first."

Historical Receipt: The Arch of Titus

This historical relief is the visual proof of the End of the Law. The physical instruments of the Law—the Menorah and the Table of Showbread—were carried into exile, never to return to a functioning Mosaic system. This was the public manifestation that the "first" had been taken away.

3. The Dissolution of the Elements (Stoicheia)

In 2 Peter 3, the "elements" (stoicheia) that melt with fervent heat are not atoms or molecules. In the Greek of the New Testament, stoicheia consistently refers to the **elementary principles of the Law**.

  • Galatians 4:3, 9: Paul refers to the Law as "weak and miserable elementary principles."
  • Colossians 2:8, 20: He warns against being subject to the "elements of the world."

The "melting" was the fiery judgment of AD 70 that dissolved the religious building blocks of the Mosaic age, leaving only the "unshakable" New Covenant reality.

4. Living Outside the Law

The "End of the Law" means that your standing with God is no longer managed by a system of distance, ritual, or performance. You do not live under a covenant that is "ready to vanish" (Heb 8:13); you live in a Kingdom that has already been established.

The Reality: If the Law ended in AD 70, then the "accuser of the brothers" lost his legal standing. There is no Law to prosecute you, and no Temple to keep you out. Access is finished. Presence is constant.
