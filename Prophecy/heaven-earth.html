Heaven and Earth

Covenant World Language · Not Physical Cosmology

In Scripture, “heaven and earth” is not primarily astrophysical language. It is covenant language. It describes an ordered world of authority, worship, law, and identity — not the material universe.

When the Bible speaks of heaven and earth being created, shaken, worn out, or replaced, it is speaking about the **rise and fall of covenant worlds**.

1. Heaven and Earth in the Old Testament

Israel’s prophets regularly used “heaven and earth” to describe the covenant order governing Israel’s life.

  • Heaven — ruling authority, priesthood, leadership
  • Earth — the people living under that order

This language appears in covenant lawsuits, warnings, and restorations. It is symbolic, relational, and legal — not scientific.

Example:

  • Isaiah 1:2 — “Hear, O heavens, and give ear, O earth”

The prophet is not addressing planets. He is calling the covenant order to account.

2. Creation and De-Creation Language

Prophets describe covenant collapse using “creation reversal” imagery:

  • sun darkened
  • stars falling
  • earth shaking
  • heaven rolled up

This language appears in:

  • Isaiah 13 (Babylon)
  • Isaiah 34 (Edom)
  • Ezekiel 32 (Egypt)

None of these passages describe the end of the physical universe. They describe the end of **political–covenantal worlds**.

3. Jesus and “Heaven and Earth”

Jesus stands firmly in this prophetic tradition. When He says:

  • “Heaven and earth will pass away” (Matt 24:35)

He is not predicting planetary destruction. He is announcing the end of the Mosaic covenant order.

This fits the context:

  • temple judgment
  • that generation
  • Jerusalem’s destruction

Jesus is declaring covenant replacement, not cosmic extinction.

4. Hebrews: Shaking Heaven and Earth

Hebrews 12 explicitly defines the meaning:

  • “Yet once more” indicates the removal of what can be shaken
  • so that what cannot be shaken may remain

The author explains this is the removal of the old covenant system, not the destruction of the material creation.

The result is:

  • an unshakable kingdom
  • established access
  • covenant stability

5. 2 Peter 3 — Heavens and Earth Replaced

Peter uses the same covenant framework. He compares:

  • the world before the Flood
  • the present heavens and earth

Both refer to covenant orders. The “new heavens and new earth” promise comes directly from Isaiah and refers to New Covenant life, not a new planet.

6. New Creation Reality

The New Testament does not leave this unresolved. It declares:

  • the old heaven and earth have passed
  • a new covenant world is established
  • access to God is permanent

Believers live in the New Creation now — not waiting for it, but participating in it.

Key rule: Never read “heaven and earth” literally when Scripture itself defines it covenantally.
