Is the "Second Coming" a Biblical Phrase?

Examining the gap between modern tradition and the first-century covenantal vocabulary.

Linguistic Analysis Hebrews 9:28 Covenant Transition

1. The Silence of Scripture & The Trap of the Label

If you were to open a concordance and search for the specific term "Second Coming," you would find that the Holy Spirit never saw fit to use it. What is in the Bible is the promise that He would "appear a second time" (Hebrews 9:28).

By naming the event the "Second Coming," tradition created a theology of absence—picturing Christ as a King who went away on a long vacation and will eventually return in a biological body. However, the New Testament writers were not concerned with a return; they were concerned with a manifestation.

2. Understanding the Greek Vocabulary

The Apostles used three specific Greek words to describe the climax of the age, none of which carry the baggage of "coming back".

A) Parousia (παρουσία) — Presence

Literally "being alongside." It describes a state of presence. In the first century, it specifically referred to the "royal visitation" of a King to a city—the arrival of his authority to judge, reward, and reorder the government.

B) Apocalypsis (ἀποκάλυψις) — Unveiling

The pulling back of a curtain. It suggests that Christ was already there, but hidden from sight behind the "veil" of the Old Covenant system.

C) Epiphaneia (ἐπιφάνεια) — Manifestation

A "shining forth." The moment a light is turned on in a dark room. The room didn't change; the light simply made the reality visible.

3. The High Priest Logic (Hebrews 9:28)

Hebrews 9:28 is the only verse in the Bible that uses the word "second" in relation to Christ appearing. In its 1st-century context, this was Day of Atonement code.

"...so Christ also, having been offered once to bear the sins of many, will appear a second time, without sin, to those who are eagerly waiting for him for salvation."

On the Day of Atonement, the High Priest would:

  • First Appearance: Appear before the people to take the sacrifice.
  • The Absence: Disappear behind the veil into the Holy of Holies to present the blood.
  • Second Appearance: Emerge from behind the veil to show the people the sacrifice was accepted.

Hebrews 9 argues that Christ's "second appearing" was His emergence from the "heavenly sanctuary" to officially end the Old Covenant age. This was the liturgical completion of the Atonement that happened in AD 70.

Historical Reality: The End of the Sanctuary

Historical Proof: This relief from the Arch of Titus depicts the physical vessels of the Temple being carried away in AD 70. This is the visual confirmation of Hebrews 9:8—"the way into the Holy Place wasn’t yet revealed while the first tabernacle was still standing." When the physical sanctuary fell, the High Priest's "second appearance" was finalized in history.

4. Shadow vs. Substance: The End of the Law

The Parousia (Presence) of Christ is not about a physical body touching down in the Middle East; it is about the Covenantal Takeover.

As long as the Old Covenant Temple stood, the "way" into God's presence was symbolically restricted. The "appearing" of Christ in AD 70 was the public removal of that entire system.

"He takes away the first, that he may establish the second."
— Hebrews 10:9

We do not look for a physical King on a physical throne because that would drag the New Covenant backward into the shadows of the Old Covenant. The goal of the cross was the establishment of His permanent, unmediated presence with His Church everywhere.

5. The Vindication of the Martyrs

The "coming" of Christ was also the legal resolution of a 40-year court case. Jesus promised that all the righteous blood shed on the earth would be required of "this generation" (Matt 23:35-36). The Parousia was the moment the Judge entered the courtroom, vindicated His people, and passed sentence on the persecutors in AD 70.

6. Why It Matters Today

If we are still waiting for a "Second Coming," we are essentially saying that the Atonement process is still unfinished—that our High Priest is still "stuck" in the sanctuary.

But the fulfilled view recognizes that the High Priest has emerged. The Parousia has been manifested. The Old World is gone, and we now live in the unshakable Kingdom (Hebrews 12:28).

The Shift: Tradition says "He is coming." Scripture says "He is here." New Creation life begins when you stop looking at the horizon for a rescue and start looking at the Throne for your current reality.
