Luke 8

Dominion Over Chaos • The Legions Evicted • The End of Death

1. Immediate Context

Luke 8 presents an escalating series of demonstrations regarding the Son’s jurisdictional power. Having established His authority over disease and religious debt in previous chapters, the Messiah now moves to silence the chaotic forces of the old world. During the AD 30–70 transition, these events served as definitive proof that the New Creation was not merely a spiritual concept but a sovereign reality with the power to subdue the elements, the demonic, and the grave.

The chapter opens with the Parable of the Sower, which defines the expansion of the Kingdom through the Word rather than the political or ritualistic mechanisms of the past. As the narrative progresses toward the AD 70 horizon, the silencing of the storm and the eviction of the "Legion" in Gentile territory signal that the Old Covenant shadows are being swept away. The chapter culminates in the restoration of Jairus’s daughter, proving that the New World is characterized by an indestructible life that has already legally triumphed over the Adamic curse.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/LUK08.htm

1 It happened soon afterwards, that he went about through cities and villages, preaching and bringing the good news of God’s Kingdom. With him were the twelve, 2 and certain women who had been healed of evil spirits and infirmities: Mary who was called Magdalene, from whom seven demons had gone out; 3 and Joanna, the wife of Chuzas, Herod’s steward; Susanna; and many others; who served them from their possessions.
4 When a great multitude came together, and people from every city were coming to him, he spoke by a parable. 5 “The sower went out to sow his seed. As he sowed, some fell by the roadside, and it was trodden under foot, and the birds of the sky devoured it. 6 Other seed fell on the rock, and as soon as it grew, it withered away, because it had no moisture. 7 Other seed fell among the thorns, and the thorns grew with it, and choked it. 8 Other seed fell into the good ground, and grew, and produced one hundred times as much fruit.” As he said these things, he called out, “He who has ears to hear, let him hear!”
9 Then his disciples asked him, “What does this parable mean?” 10 He said, “To you it is given to know the mysteries of God’s Kingdom, but to the rest in parables; that ‘seeing they may not see, and hearing they may not understand.’ 11 Now the parable is this: The seed is the word of God. 12 Those by the roadside are those who hear, then the devil comes, and takes away the word from their heart, that they may not believe and be saved. 13 Those on the rock are they who, when they hear, receive the word with joy; but these have no root, who believe for a while, then fall away in time of temptation. 14 That which fell among the thorns, these are those who have heard, and as they go on their way they are choked with cares, riches, and pleasures of life, and bring no fruit to perfection. 15 That in the good ground, these are such as in an honest and good heart, having heard the word, hold it tightly, and produce fruit with patience.
16 “No one, when he has lit a lamp, covers it with a container, or puts it under a bed; but puts it on a stand, that those who enter in may see the light. 17 For nothing is hidden, that will not be revealed; nor anything secret, that will not be known and come into the light. 18 Be careful therefore how you hear. For whoever has, to him will be given; and whoever doesn’t have, from him will be taken away even that which he thinks he has.”
19 His mother and his brothers came to him, and they could not come near him for the crowd. 20 It was told him by some saying, “Your mother and your brothers are standing outside, desiring to see you.” 21 But he answered them, “My mother and my brothers are these who hear the word of God, and do it.”
22 Now it happened on one of those days, that he entered into a boat, himself and his disciples, and he said to them, “Let’s go over to the other side of the lake.” So they launched out. 23 But as they sailed, he fell asleep. A wind storm came down on the lake, and they were filling with water, and were in danger. 24 They came to him, and awoke him, saying, “Master, master, we are perishing!” He awoke, and rebuked the wind and the raging of the water, and they ceased, and it was calm. 25 He said to them, “Where is your faith?” Being afraid they marveled, saying one to another, “Who is this, then, that he commands even the winds and the water, and they obey him?”
26 They arrived at the country of the Gadarenes, which is opposite Galilee. 27 When he stepped ashore, a certain man out of the city who had demons for a long time met him. He wore no clothes, and didn’t live in a house, but in the tombs. 28 When he saw Jesus, he cried out, and fell down before him, and with a loud voice said, “What have I to do with you, Jesus, Son of the Most High God? I beg you, don’t torment me!” 29 For he was commanding the unclean spirit to come out of the man. For the unclean spirit had often seized him. He was kept under guard, and bound with chains and fetters. Breaking the bands apart, he was driven by the demon into the desert. 30 Jesus asked him, “What is your name?” He said, “Legion,” for many demons had entered into him. 31 They begged him that he would not command them to go into the abyss. 32 Now there was a herd of many pigs feeding on the mountain, and they begged him that he would allow them to enter into them. He allowed them. 33 The demons went out from the man, and entered into the pigs, and the herd rushed down the steep bank into the lake, and were drowned. 34 When those who fed them saw what had happened, they fled, and told it in the city and in the country. 35 The people went out to see what had happened. They came to Jesus, and found the man from whom the demons had gone out, sitting at Jesus’ feet, clothed and in his right mind; and they were afraid. 36 Those who saw it told them how he who was possessed by demons was healed. 37 All the people of the surrounding country of the Gadarenes asked him to depart from them, for they were very much afraid. He entered into a boat, and returned. 38 But the man from whom the demons had gone out begged him that he might go with him. But Jesus sent him away, saying, 39 “Return to your house, and declare what great things God has done for you.” He went his way, proclaiming throughout the whole city what great things Jesus had done for him.
40 It happened, when Jesus returned, the multitude welcomed him, for they were all waiting for him. 41 Behold, there came a man named Jairus, and he was a ruler of the synagogue. He fell down at Jesus’ feet, and begged him to come into his house, 42 for he had an only daughter, about twelve years old, and she was dying. But as he went, the multitudes pressed against him.
43 A woman who had an issue of blood for twelve years, and had spent all her living on physicians, and could not be healed by any, 44 came behind him, and touched the fringe of his cloak, and immediately the flow of her blood stopped. 45 Jesus said, “Who touched me?” When all denied it, Peter and those with him said, “Master, the multitudes press and jostle you, and you say, ‘Who touched me?’” 46 But Jesus said, “Someone did touch me, for I perceived that power had gone out from me.” 47 When the woman saw that she was not hidden, she came trembling, and falling down before him declared to him in the presence of all the people the reason why she had touched him, and how she was healed immediately. 48 He said to her, “Daughter, cheer up. Your faith has made you well. Go in peace.”
49 While he was still speaking, someone came from the ruler of the synagogue’s house, saying to him, “Your daughter is dead. Don’t trouble the Teacher.” 50 But Jesus hearing it, answered him, “Don’t be afraid. Only believe, and she will be made well.” 51 When he came to the house, he didn’t allow anyone to enter in, except Peter, John, James, the father of the child, and her mother. 52 All were weeping and mourning her, but he said, “Don’t weep, for she isn’t dead, but asleep.” 53 They laughed him to scorn, knowing that she was dead. 54 But he, putting them all outside, took her by the hand and called, saying, “Child, arise!” 55 Her spirit returned, and she rose up immediately. He commanded that something be given her to eat. 56 Her parents were amazed, but he commanded them to tell no one what had been done.

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • Mysteries of the Kingdom (v. 10): Greek mystēria. These are the formerly hidden legal aspects of the New Creation that were being unveiled exclusively to the remnant of the transition generation.
  • What Have I to Do with You? (v. 28): A legal challenge from the demonic realm. The legion recognizes the Son’s jurisdictional right to judge and evict the powers of the old world before the final foreclosure of the age.
  • Twelve Years (vv. 42-43): A numerical bridge linking the daughter of the synagogue ruler and the woman with the flow of blood. This highlights the terminal state of "Daughter Israel" under the Law and the Son's power to restore life where the old system failed.
  • Your Faith has Made You Well (v. 48): Sesōken. Faith is recognized as the legal mechanism of the New Creation, allowing the individual to bypass the professional mediators of the old age and receive direct restoration.

4. Annotations

  • The Sea / The Lake (v. 22) — In Hebrew prophecy, the sea represents the chaotic Gentile nations and the restless abyss. Silencing the storm is a manifest claim of mastery over the global theater of the New World.
  • The Abyss (v. 31) — The place of containment for the spiritual forces governing the old world order. The demons acknowledge the Son’s power to terminate their legal jurisdiction.
  • Fringe of His Cloak (v. 44) — The tzitzit. Legally required by Numbers 15:38 as a reminder of the Law. The woman’s healing through the fringe signifies that the Son has perfectly satisfied the Law's requirements and now radiates its intended life.
  • Asleep (v. 52) — A sovereign redefinition. In the presence of the Source of Life, the "death" of the old age loses its legal finality and becomes a state subject to His command.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

1) Manifest Glory Over the Chaotic Elements

In the silencing of the storm (Luke 8:24), the Son’s glory is unveiled as a power that commands the very fabric of creation. The disciples' question—"Who is this?"—reveals that they were standing before the Sovereign who legally owns both the "Heavens and the Earth." While the old world was defined by the chaos of the curse, the New Creation is established by the peace of the Son's word. This event was a definitive legal notification to the transition generation that the natural order must bow to the advent of the Kingdom. The calm that followed His rebuke was the first-century foretaste of the permanent rest that characterizes the New World after the old system was cleared in AD 70. This dominion is not a future promise but an active jurisdiction already at work in the midst of the storm.

2) The Eviction of the Old World Legions

The encounter with the Gerasene demoniac (Luke 8:27-35) is a dramatic legal repossession of humanity. The man, living among tombs and bound by chains, was the physical embodiment of the Adamic curse and the bondage of the old age. The name "Legion" links the demonic occupation to the military and political structures of the world that was passing away. By commanding the spirits into the pigs and then into the "deep," the Son performed a visual enactment of the coming judgment. He was clearing the Land of the spiritual squatters who had usurped authority over God's people. The man "clothed and in his right mind" is the ultimate picture of the New Creation humanity: restored, sane, and resting at the feet of the Sovereign, proving that the King's Word is the only true power of emancipation.

3) The Cleansing of the Blood: Bypassing the Law's Restriction

The healing of the woman with the issue of blood (Luke 8:43-48) marks the legal end of religious exclusion. For twelve years—the lifespan of the synagogue ruler’s daughter—this woman was "unclean," barred from the Temple and the community by the technicalities of the Law. When she touched the fringe of His cloak, she was not merely seeking a miracle; she was appealing to the One who perfectly fulfilled the Law. Instead of His glory being defiled by her touch, His life consumed her disease. This act proves that the New Creation operates by a higher Law: the Law of Life. The Son’s public recognition of her "faith" legally established her status as a "Daughter" of the Kingdom, bypassing the professional mediators of the old age and granting her direct, permanent access to His peace.

4) Authority Over the Grave: Death as Sleep

The raising of Jairus’s daughter (Luke 8:49-56) is the final demonstration in this chapter of the Son's glory over the terminal consequences of the first Adam. While the world mourned a "dead" child, the Son redefined her state as "asleep." This was not a poetic metaphor but a jurisdictional decree. In the presence of the Prince of Life, death loses its legal finality. By putting the mockers outside and taking the child by the hand, the Son demonstrated that the New Creation is an exclusive realm of life that is not subject to the scoffs of the old religious order. This restoration of the 12-year-old girl signifies that the True Israel—the Royal Priesthood—lives through His touch, even as the institutional structures of the synagogue were reaching their historical end. The grave has been legally defeated in the presence of the Sovereign.

8. New Creation Reality

In Luke 8, the Son’s glory is revealed as a presence that quiets every storm and evicts every "legion" of fear. As a citizen of the New Creation, you are the man sitting at His feet, clothed and in your right mind. You do not live in a world governed by the chaotic forces of the past or the chains of religious performance. The "Deep" has been silenced, and the "Legion" has been drowned. Your life is founded on the Word that commands death to become "sleep." You are invited to "cheer up" and "go in peace," knowing that the Sovereign touch has already made you well. The transition is finished; you inhabit a world where His authority is the established atmosphere of your permanent rest.

9. Reflection

Question: If the Son commanded the winds, evicted the legions, and raised the dead by His word alone, why do we still act as though the "chaos" of the world or the "demons" of our past have a legal right to disturb our rest in the New Creation?
