The AD 70 Covenant Transition

The End of an Age

The "end of the world" frequently discussed in the New Testament was not the end of the physical globe, but the sunteleia tou aiōnos—the "completion of the age." This refers specifically to the Old Covenant age centered on the physical Temple in Jerusalem. In AD 70, when the Roman legions burned the Temple, the "heavens and earth" of the Jewish religious world passed away.

The Full Establishment of the New

From the cross (AD 30) to the destruction of the Temple (AD 70), there was a 40-year period of "covenantal overlap." During this time, the New Covenant was legally active but the Old was "obsolete and drawing near to vanishing" (Hebrews 8:13). The events of AD 70 served as the definitive, historical vindication of Jesus Christ as the true King and the Church as the true Temple. We now live in the fully realized New Creation, where God dwells with man spiritually and permanently.
