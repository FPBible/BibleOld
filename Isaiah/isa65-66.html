Isaiah

Chapters 65–66 — New Heavens, New Earth, New Covenant People

God Sought by the Nations

I was ready to be sought by those who did not ask for Me; I was ready to be found by those who did not seek Me. I said, “Here I am, here I am,” to a nation that was not called by My name. (Isa 65:1)

Old Covenant Resistance

I spread out My hands all the day to a rebellious people, who walk in a way that is not good, following their own devices. (Isa 65:2)

A New Creation Announced

“For behold, I create new heavens and a new earth, and the former things shall not be remembered or come into mind. But be glad and rejoice forever in that which I create.” (Isa 65:17–18)

No more shall there be in it an infant who lives but a few days, or an old man who does not fill out his days; for the young man shall die a hundred years old, and the sinner a hundred years old shall be accursed. (Isa 65:20)

Peace and Restoration

They shall build houses and inhabit them; they shall plant vineyards and eat their fruit. They shall not build and another inhabit; they shall not plant and another eat. (Isa 65:21–22)

The wolf and the lamb shall graze together; the lion shall eat straw like the ox; they shall not hurt or destroy in all My holy mountain. (Isa 65:25)

True Worship Redefined

Thus says the LORD: “Heaven is My throne, and the earth is My footstool. What is the house that you would build for Me? All these things My hand has made.” (Isa 66:1–2)

The Birth of a New People

Before she was in labour she gave birth; before her pain came upon her she delivered a son. Shall a land be born in one day? Shall a nation be brought forth in one moment? (Isa 66:7–8)

Worldwide Gathering

“I will gather all nations and tongues, and they shall come and see My glory. They shall declare My glory among the nations.” (Isa 66:18–19)

From new moon to new moon, and from Sabbath to Sabbath, all flesh shall come to worship before Me, declares the LORD. (Isa 66:23)

Fulfilled Commentary — Isaiah 65–66

“New heavens and new earth” is **covenantal language**, not astrophysics. Isaiah describes a **new order of life**, not a new planet. Death still exists, time still passes — proving this is not the end of physical reality, but the end of an old covenant world.

The chapter contrasts two groups: those clinging to old religious identity and those God calls from outside expected boundaries. This is fulfilled as Gentiles enter covenant life while old-covenant resistance collapses.

Temple language is dismantled. God no longer locates Himself in buildings, but among the humble and receptive. This anticipates the New Covenant reality where God dwells in people.

The sudden birth of a nation is fulfilled in the emergence of the Church — a people brought forth not by law, but by promise.

Isaiah ends not with destruction, but with **global worship, peace, and continuity**. The New Creation is stable, enduring, and centred on God’s presence with His people.
