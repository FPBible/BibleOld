Zechariah 5

The Curse of the Scroll and the Relocation of Wickedness

1. Immediate Context

After a series of comforting visions depicting the continuous power of the Spirit and the cleansing of the priesthood, Zechariah 5 presents a severe prophetic pivot. The visions of the Flying Scroll and the Woman in the Ephah (Basket) introduce the inevitable covenantal judgment that must fall upon the apostate nation. For the true New Covenant temple to be fully established, the corrupt, rebellious system of the Old Covenant must be completely removed. This chapter vividly maps out the first-century climax, detailing how the curse of the Law would literally consume the physical temple and how God would officially identify apostate Jerusalem as "Mystery Babylon"—exiling the harlot system permanently to make way for the New Jerusalem.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/ZEC05.htm

1 I lifted up my eyes again, and saw, and behold, a flying scroll.
2 He said to me, "What do you see?" I answered, "I see a flying scroll; its length is twenty cubits, and its width ten cubits."
3 Then he said to me, "This is the curse that goes out over the surface of the whole land; for everyone who steals shall be cut off according to it on the one side; and everyone who swears falsely shall be cut off according to it on the other side.
4 I will cause it to go out," says Yahweh of Armies, "and it will enter into the house of the thief, and into the house of him who swears falsely by my name; and it will remain in the middle of his house, and will destroy it with its timber and its stones."
5 Then the angel who talked with me came forward, and said to me, "Lift up now your eyes, and see what this is that is appearing."
6 I said, "What is it?" He said, "This is the ephah basket that is appearing." He said moreover, "This is their appearance in all the land
7 (and behold, a lead cover was lifted up); and there was a woman sitting in the middle of the ephah basket."
8 He said, "This is Wickedness;" and he threw her down into the middle of the ephah basket; and he threw the lead weight on its mouth.
9 Then I lifted up my eyes, and saw, and behold, there were two women, and the wind was in their wings. Now they had wings like the wings of a stork, and they lifted up the ephah basket between earth and the sky.
10 Then I said to the angel who talked with me, "Where are these carrying the ephah basket?"
11 He said to me, "To build her a house in the land of Shinar. When it is prepared, she will be set there in her own place."

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • The Consumption of Timber and Stones: A direct prophetic precursor to Jesus' Olivet Discourse, where He declared that not one stone of the temple would be left upon another (Matthew 24:2), completely fulfilled in AD 70.
  • The Curse of the Law: The scroll represents the covenantal sanctions of Deuteronomy 28 falling upon the final generation of the Old Covenant age who rejected the Messiah.
  • The Relocation to Shinar: Shinar is the ancient name for Babylon. This establishes the apocalyptic typological link where apostate, first-century Jerusalem is spiritually identified and judged as "Mystery Babylon" (Revelation 17-18).

4. Annotations

  • Twenty Cubits by Ten Cubits: These are the exact dimensions of the Holy Place in the tabernacle, as well as the porch of Solomon's temple. It signifies that this judgment is measured by divine standards and begins at the house of God.
  • The Curse: The sworn covenantal wrath guaranteed by God against His people if they continually broke the Mosaic Law.
  • Ephah Basket: The largest dry measure in ancient Israel, often associated with commerce and the marketplace. Here, it symbolizes the full, accumulated measure of the nation's iniquity.
  • Woman named Wickedness: The personification of apostasy and idolatry. In apocalyptic language, this is the "Harlot"—the unfaithful covenant bride who aligns with the world.
  • Land of Shinar: The geographical origin of the Tower of Babel (Genesis 11). Prophetically, it represents the ultimate state of rebellion and confusion standing in opposition to the City of God.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

The Dimensions of Divine Judgment (Verses 1-2)

Zechariah's sixth vision is terrifying in its scale. He sees a massive flying scroll, roughly thirty feet long and fifteen feet wide. These dimensions are not arbitrary; they perfectly match the measurements of the Holy Place in the tabernacle (Exodus 26) and the porch of Solomon's temple (1 Kings 6:3). This detail is critical for fulfilled eschatology.

The scroll represents the very standard of God's sanctuary going out as a prosecuting force. It signifies that judgment is not coming upon random pagan nations, but rather it is beginning at the house of God. The Old Covenant system, which was meant to be a source of life and mediation, had become a ministry of death and condemnation due to the rebellion of the people. The standard of the temple itself was now flying over the land to execute divine justice.

The Curse of the Broken Covenant (Verses 3-4a)

The angel explicitly identifies the scroll as "the curse that goes out over the surface of the whole land." The scroll targets the thief and the one who swears falsely, crimes that represent the two tablets of the Decalogue (sins against man and sins against God). Under the Mosaic Law, obedience brought blessings, but persistent covenant-breaking brought the curses outlined in Deuteronomy 28.

By the first century, the Apostle Paul recognized this theological reality, declaring that "as many as are of the works of the law are under a curse" (Galatians 3:10). The apostate religious leaders of Jesus' day possessed the scroll of the Law, but rather than finding salvation in it, it became their warrant for execution. Because they rejected the Branch (Christ) who came to bear the curse for them, the covenantal curse was unleashed upon their generation.

The Consumption of Timber and Stones (Verse 4b)

The flying scroll does not merely judge individuals; God declares that it will remain in the middle of the house and "destroy it with its timber and its stones." This is one of the most stunningly precise prophecies of the AD 70 covenant transition.

When Jesus departed the physical temple for the final time in Matthew 23 and 24, He pronounced the house "desolate." His disciples marveled at the massive timber and stones of the Herodian structure, but Jesus replied, "There will not be left here one stone on another, that will not be thrown down." The Roman armies were merely the physical instruments; it was the covenantal curse of the flying scroll that completely consumed the timber and stones of the Old Covenant system, clearing the ground for the indestructible New Creation temple.

Filling Up the Measure of Sin (Verses 5-8)

The seventh vision introduces an ephah basket with a woman sitting inside, identified as "Wickedness." The ephah was a unit of measure. In apocalyptic imagery, God is showing Zechariah that the iniquity of the apostate nation was being measured and contained. The lead weight thrown over the mouth of the basket signifies that her judgment is sealed and inescapable.

Jesus utilized this exact conceptual framework when confronting the Pharisees. He told them, "Fill up, then, the measure of your fathers!" (Matthew 23:32). The first-century generation was the one that would fill the "ephah" of covenantal rebellion to the brim by crucifying the Lord of Glory and persecuting His Church. The woman in the basket is the Old Covenant Harlot, the unfaithful system that had utterly corrupted the land.

The Flight to Spiritual Babylon (Verses 9-11)

Two women with stork wings lift the basket and carry it away to "the land of Shinar" to build her a house there. Shinar is ancient Babylon. This is a profound typological inversion. Historically, God's people were taken captive to Babylon. But prophetically, God is declaring that the apostate covenant system itself has become Babylon.

This is the interpretive key to the Book of Revelation. When John sees the great Harlot city, he calls it "Mystery Babylon" (Revelation 17:5). It was the city where the Lord was crucified (Revelation 11:8). God removed the title of "Holy City" from earthly Jerusalem, exiled its spiritual identity to the realm of rebellion (Shinar), and gave it over to destruction. This permanent separation allowed the true Bride, the New Jerusalem, to emerge flawlessly from heaven, free from the wicked house of the old order.

8. New Creation Reality

The heavy curse of the scroll and the consuming judgment of the law fell definitively upon the old world so that you could live entirely in the new. The New Creation reality is that Christ has borne the curse of the scroll on your behalf, completely relocating you from the doomed house of timber and stones into the eternal, secure sanctuary of His grace.
