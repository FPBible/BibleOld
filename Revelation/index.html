Revelation

Apocalyptic Prophecy · Covenant Judgment · New Creation Unveiled

First-Century Fulfillment AD 70 Destruction Victory of the Bride

Orientation

Revelation is apocalyptic covenant prophecy written to first-century churches under intense pressure. It unveils Jesus as the present King, judges the persecuting Old Covenant order, and announces the public arrival of the New Covenant reality.

This book is not an end-times chart factory. It is a time-framed, audience-locked message of endurance, discernment, and vindication. The churches were tempted to fear, blend in, or bow to Rome and apostate Jerusalem. Revelation does not offer escapism; it gives Christ-centered clarity. It uses prophetic idioms to declare covenant judgment on the old world, leaving the New Creation people standing in the Lamb’s victory.
