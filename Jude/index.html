Jude

Contending for the Faith · The Judgment of the Apostates · Once for All Delivered

Jude is the shortest letter in the New Testament and one of the most concentrated in eschatological urgency. Jude had intended to write about the common salvation, but the infiltration of apostates forced a different document.

This letter is written into the final years of the Old Covenant age. Its judgments are first-century, its audience is specific, and its doxology is timeless.
