The Living Temple

1 Peter 2:4-9 • Rejection, Election, and the New Priesthood

Cornerstone Holy Nation AD 70 Eviction

1. The Rejected Stone

Peter identifies Jesus as the "Living Stone." This is the primary legal collision of the first century. To the "builders" (the Old Covenant leadership in Jerusalem), He was a stumbling block. To God, He was the chosen and precious Cornerstone of a new administration.

"For it is contained in Scripture, 'Behold, I lay in Zion a chief cornerstone, chosen, and precious: He who believes in him will not be disappointed.'" (v. 6, WEB)

The "builders" rejected the stone in AD 30, but in AD 70, the Stone fell on them (Matthew 21:44). The destruction of the physical Temple was the historical proof that the "builders" had lost their contract.

2. You Are the House

Peter tells the Diaspora believers that they are the Temple. This was a radical jurisdictional shift. As long as the physical Temple stood, it claimed to be the exclusive place of God's presence. Peter declares that "the way" is now manifested in a spiritual house.

You are "living stones," being built up into a spiritual house to be a holy priesthood. The Levitical priesthood was tied to biological descent and physical animal sacrifice. The New Priesthood is tied to union with Christ and the "sacrifice" of Spirit-led life. One was vanishing; the other is eternal.

The Royal Decree

Peter applies the titles of Israel to the New Covenant people. This is the official Takeover of the covenant identity.

"But you are a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, a people for God’s own possession..." (v. 9)

The shadow (Physical Israel/Temple) has been removed. The substance (The Royal Priesthood) remains.
