Preface to 1 Peter

The End of All Things is at Hand

The Historical Context

1 Peter was written to the "exiles of the Dispersion"—Jewish and Gentile believers living in the final decade of the Old Covenant age. They were the transition generation, the "chosen generation" called to witness the final "unveiling" of Jesus Christ in the destruction of the Christ-rejecting Judean system.

The Great Horizon

Peter’s eschatology is strictly immediate. He does not point to a distant 21st-century future. He tells his readers:

  • Salvation was "ready to be revealed in the last time" (1:5).
  • The prophets were not serving themselves, but "you" (1:12).
  • The end of all things "is at hand" (4:7).
  • The time had come for judgment to "begin at the house of God" (4:17).

This module handles 1 Peter not as a manual for a postponed hope, but as a survival guide for the first-century saints as they moved from the "Shadow" of the Temple into the "Substance" of the New Creation.
