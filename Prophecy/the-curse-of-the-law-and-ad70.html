The Flying Scroll: The Curse Unleashed

The Legal Basis of Judgment

The "flying scroll" of Zechariah 5 is explicitly identified as "the curse." This refers to the covenantal sanctions established in Deuteronomy 28. If Israel obeyed, they would be blessed; if they turned to apostasy, they would be cursed with exile, plague, and total destruction. The scroll was the "warrant" for the nation's execution.

The Climax of the Generation

Jesus warned that all the righteous blood shed on earth—from Abel to Zechariah—would come upon "this generation" (Matthew 23:36). By crucifying the Messiah and the apostles, the first-century religious establishment filled the "ephah" of iniquity. This forced the "flying scroll" of the Law to finally execute its full, terrifying penalty. The events of AD 70 were the literal acting out of the curses of the Law upon the unfaithful covenant partner.

Redeemed from the Curse

While the old system was being consumed by the curse of the scroll, the believing remnant was safe. Paul declared that "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law, having become a curse for us" (Galatians 3:13). In the New Creation, the scroll of judgment is closed, and we live under the "spirit of grace," completely immune to the covenantal wrath that fell upon the old world.
