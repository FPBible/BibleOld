Zechariah 13

The Opened Fountain and the Refining of the Remnant

1. Immediate Context

Following the outpouring of the Spirit of grace and the mourning for the Pierced One in Chapter 12, Zechariah 13 details the immediate, saving results of that crucifixion. The death of the Messiah forcefully opens an eternal fountain of cleansing, decisively removing the need for the repetitive animal sacrifices of the Old Covenant. This chapter graphically outlines the covenantal transition: the final purging of false prophets, the divine striking of the True Shepherd, and the agonizing but necessary fiery tribulation that would strike first-century Judea. This tribulation permanently destroyed the apostate majority while perfectly refining the believing remnant—the early Church—who emerged as the fully recognized citizens of the New Creation.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/ZEC13.htm

1 "In that day there will be a fountain opened to the house of David and to the inhabitants of Jerusalem, for sin and for uncleanness.
2 It will come to pass in that day, says Yahweh of Armies, that I will cut off the names of the idols out of the land, and they will be remembered no more. I will also cause the prophets and the spirit of impurity to pass out of the land.
3 It will happen that, when anyone still prophesies, then his father and his mother who bore him will tell him, 'You must die, because you speak lies in the name of Yahweh;' and his father and his mother who bore him will stab him when he prophesies.
4 It will happen in that day, that the prophets will each be ashamed of his vision, when he prophesies; neither will they wear a hairy mantle to deceive:
5 but he will say, 'I am no prophet, I am a tiller of the ground; for I have been made a bondservant from my youth.'
6 One will say to him, 'What are these wounds between your arms?' Then he will answer, 'Those with which I was wounded in the house of my friends.'
7 'Awake, sword, against my shepherd, and against the man who is close to me,' says Yahweh of Armies. 'Strike the shepherd, and the sheep will be scattered; and I will turn my hand against the little ones.
8 It shall happen that in all the land,' says Yahweh, 'two parts in it will be cut off and die; but the third will be left in it.
9 I will bring the third part into the fire, and will refine them as silver is refined, and will test them like gold is tested. They will call on my name, and I will hear them. I will say, "It is my people;" and they will say, "Yahweh is my God."'"

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • The Fountain Opened: Fulfilled perfectly at the cross of Christ, specifically when His side was pierced and blood and water flowed out (John 19:34), providing final, permanent atonement.
  • Striking the Shepherd: Explicitly quoted by Jesus in Matthew 26:31 just hours before His arrest, confirming that the "scattering of the sheep" referred directly to His disciples abandoning Him in Gethsemane.
  • The Refining Fire: Refers to the severe first-century tribulations, persecutions, and the Jewish-Roman War, which tested the faith of the early Church (1 Peter 1:6-7) before the end of the age.
  • Two Parts Cut Off: Historically realized in the catastrophic AD 70 judgment upon apostate Judea, where the vast majority of the nation was destroyed, leaving only the "third part" (the believing Christian remnant) to inherit the New Covenant promises.

4. Annotations

  • Fountain: A continuous, living source of water, contrasting with stagnant cisterns or the repetitive, temporary basins of the Levitical system.
  • Spirit of Impurity: The demonic, adversarial forces driving false religion and false prophecy under the Old Covenant era.
  • Hairy Mantle: The traditional rough garment worn by true prophets like Elijah and John the Baptist; here, it is hypocritically worn by false prophets to deceive the people.
  • Wounds between your arms (hands): Contextually refers to the self-inflicted ritual cuttings of false pagan prophets (like the prophets of Baal). However, it serves as a profound typological shadow of Christ's crucifixion wounds received in the "house of His friends" (Jerusalem).
  • The Man who is close to me: Literally "the man who is my equal" or "my companion." A stunning declaration of the deity of the Shepherd (Christ) who is struck by the sword of divine justice.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

The Eternal Fountain of Cleansing (Verse 1)

Following the bitter mourning of Chapter 12, the prophecy immediately pivots to the glorious result of the cross: "In that day there will be a fountain opened... for sin and for uncleanness." Under the Mosaic Law, cleansing was a tedious, repetitive process. Blood had to be shed daily, and water of purification had to be constantly sprinkled. It was a closed, restricted system.

In the framework of fulfilled eschatology, this fountain was blasted open once and for all in the first century. When the Roman spear pierced the side of Jesus, blood and water poured out (John 19:34). The writer of Hebrews emphasizes that Christ entered the holy place once for all with His own blood, obtaining eternal redemption (Hebrews 9:12). The New Creation is defined by this reality: we do not return to physical altars because the spiritual fountain of grace is permanently open and continually cleansing.

The Purging of False Prophecy (Verses 2-5)

With the establishment of the New Covenant, God promises to radically cleanse the land of idols and "cause the prophets and the spirit of impurity to pass out of the land." The Old Covenant era was plagued by false prophets who led the nation into devastating compromises. God declares that this entire paradigm will end. The zeal for truth will be so intense that parents will execute their own children if they attempt to falsely prophesy in Yahweh's name.

This signifies the cessation of the Old Covenant prophetic office. Once the True Prophet, Jesus Christ, had spoken, and once the apostolic foundation of the Church was laid in the first century, the shadows and types were fulfilled. The false prophets would throw away their "hairy mantles" in shame, claiming to be mere farmers, terrified of the judgment. By the time Jerusalem fell in AD 70, the old system of national prophets and localized oracles was utterly dismantled, giving way to the completed revelation of Christ.

The Wounds of the False Prophet and the True Shepherd (Verse 6)

When a false prophet is interrogated about the "wounds between your arms," he desperately lies, claiming he received them in a brawl at a friend's house, trying to hide the fact that they are pagan ritual cuttings. This is the immediate context of the verse: exposing the shame of false religion.

Yet, the Holy Spirit masterfully layers a profound typological irony here. The very next verse introduces the True Shepherd. Jesus Christ, the ultimate Prophet, bore literal wounds between His hands. When He was asked about them, He could truthfully say they were received in the "house of His friends"—the covenant city of Jerusalem, by His own brethren. While the false prophets bore wounds of deception, the True Shepherd bore the wounds of redemption.

The Sword Awakened Against the Shepherd (Verse 7)

God gives a startling command: "Awake, sword, against my shepherd, and against the man who is close to me." The Shepherd is not struck by accident, nor merely by the malice of men. He is struck by the sovereign decree of Yahweh. The phrase "the man who is close to me" denotes equality, pointing to the divine nature of the Son. The Father's sword of justice, demanding satisfaction for the broken covenant, falls upon the co-equal Son.

There is zero ambiguity about the fulfillment of this text. In Matthew 26:31, as Jesus walked to the Mount of Olives on the night of His betrayal, He explicitly quoted this verse: "For it is written, 'I will strike the shepherd, and the sheep of the flock will be scattered.'" When Christ was arrested, the disciples fled in terror. The first-century cross is the absolute epicenter of Zechariah's prophetic vision.

The Fiery Refining of the First-Century Remnant (Verses 8-9)

The striking of the Shepherd initiates the final, terrifying transition of the covenantal age. Yahweh declares that in all the land, "two parts in it will be cut off and die; but the third will be left in it." The vast majority of first-century Judea hardened their hearts against the Gospel. Consequently, they were "cut off" in the horrific carnage of the AD 66-70 Jewish-Roman War.

The "third part" represents the believing Jewish remnant—the early Church. But they were not exempt from suffering; God promised to bring them "into the fire, and will refine them as silver is refined." The first-century believers endured intense persecution from both the Jewish elite and the Roman Emperor Nero (1 Peter 1:6-7). This tribulation was the crucible that burned away the dross of the Old Covenant. When the fire subsided in AD 70, this refined remnant stood as the vindicated New Creation Church, fully recognized by God who declared, "It is my people," and who triumphantly replied, "Yahweh is my God."

8. New Creation Reality

You do not need to seek out temporary purifications, nor do you need to endure the fiery covenantal wrath that fell upon the old world. The New Creation reality is that the fountain of Christ's blood is permanently open to you right now, washing you entirely clean and establishing you as God's refined and recognized people.

9. Reflection

When you face difficult "refining" seasons in your own life, how does knowing that the Shepherd was willingly struck by the sword for your salvation help you trust God's purifying process?
