In One Day: The Final Atonement

The Removal of Iniquity

One of the most startling promises in Zechariah is found in 3:9: "I will remove the iniquity of that land in one day." For the Jew, the removal of iniquity was a seasonal event—the Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur)—which had to be repeated every year because it never truly perfected the conscience of the worshiper.

The Contrast of Covenants

The "one day" of Zechariah stands in direct opposition to the "many days" of the Levitical system. Fulfilled eschatology recognizes this "one day" as the day of the Cross. On that day, the "spirit of grace" was poured out, and the "fountain for sin and uncleanness" was opened (Zechariah 13:1). The repetitive shadows of the Old Covenant were swallowed up by the finality of Christ's blood.

Present Peace

The result of this one-day removal of iniquity is peace, described in Zechariah 3:10 as everyone sitting under their vine and fig tree. This is the Sabbath Rest of the New Creation. We no longer labor under the guilt of the law or the anxiety of "incomplete" forgiveness. The Day of Atonement is over; the year of the Lord's favor is an eternal reality for the believer.
