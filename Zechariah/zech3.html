Zechariah 3

The Cleansing of the High Priest and the Coming of the Branch

1. Immediate Context

Having established the vision of the unwalled, spiritually protected city in Chapter 2, Zechariah now moves into the divine courtroom. The focus shifts from the geography of Jerusalem to the holiness of its people and priesthood. Joshua, the high priest, stands before God clothed in filth, representing the inadequacy of the Old Covenant system and the guilt of the nation. This breathtaking vision prophesies the removal of the failing Levitical priesthood and the introduction of the perfect, Melchizedekian priesthood of Christ ("the Branch"). It points directly to the cross, where the iniquity of the land would be definitively removed "in one day," completing the covenant transition in the first century.

2. Scripture (WEB)

Source (verbatim): https://ebible.org/web/ZEC03.htm

1 He showed me Joshua the high priest standing before Yahweh's angel, and Satan standing at his right hand to be his adversary.
2 Yahweh said to Satan, "Yahweh rebuke you, Satan! Yes, Yahweh who has chosen Jerusalem rebuke you! Isn't this a burning stick plucked out of the fire?"
3 Now Joshua was clothed with filthy garments, and was standing before the angel.
4 He answered and spoke to those who stood before him, saying, "Take the filthy garments off him." To him he said, "Behold, I have caused your iniquity to pass from you, and I will clothe you with rich clothing."
5 I said, "Let them set a clean turban on his head." So they set a clean turban on his head, and clothed him; and Yahweh's angel was standing by.
6 Yahweh's angel solemnly assured Joshua, saying,
7 "Yahweh of Armies says: 'If you will walk in my ways, and if you will follow my instructions, then you also shall judge my house, and shall also keep my courts, and I will give you a place of access among these who stand by.
8 Hear now, Joshua the high priest, you and your fellows who sit before you, for they are men who are a sign; for, behold, I will bring out my servant, the Branch.
9 For, behold, the stone that I have set before Joshua: on one stone are seven eyes; behold, I will engrave its inscription,' says Yahweh of Armies, 'and I will remove the iniquity of that land in one day.
10 In that day,' says Yahweh of Armies, 'you will invite every man his neighbor under the vine and under the fig tree.'"

3. Fulfillment Notes

  • The Branch: A clear, universally recognized messianic title fulfilled entirely in Jesus Christ, the ultimate King and High Priest (Jeremiah 23:5, Isaiah 11:1).
  • In One Day: A profound contrast to the continuous, repetitive annual sacrifices of the Old Covenant Day of Atonement. This points to the single, finished work of the cross (Hebrews 10:11-14).
  • Men Who Are a Sign: The physical priesthood of Zechariah's day was explicitly declared to be typological—a shadow pointing to the substantive reality of the New Covenant priesthood.

4. Annotations

  • Joshua the High Priest: The historical high priest of the returning exiles, but here functioning as the representative head of the entire Levitical system and the covenant people.
  • Filthy Garments: Apocalyptic imagery for human righteousness under the law, stained by sin and covenantal failure (often translated as excrement-stained garments, highlighting absolute defilement).
  • Satan (The Adversary): Functioning here not merely as a tempter, but as the prosecuting attorney in the divine courtroom, highlighting the legitimate legal condemnation brought by the Old Covenant law.
  • Stone with Seven Eyes: Represents Christ, the living cornerstone of the spiritual temple, possessing the fullness of the Holy Spirit (omniscience and perfect providence), an image later utilized in Revelation 5:6.

5. Fulfillment Commentary

The Legal Condemnation of the Old System (Verses 1-3)

The vision opens in a divine courtroom. Joshua the high priest stands representing the entire nation and the Old Covenant system. The shocking element is that he is clothed in "filthy garments." The high priest, who was required by law to wear pristine, holy garments to minister before God, is utterly defiled. Standing at his right hand is the adversary, bringing legitimate accusations.

Under the Old Covenant law, the adversary had a perfect case. The law brought the knowledge of sin and exposed the utter inadequacy of human righteousness. The filthy garments represent the inescapable reality that the Levitical priesthood, and the blood of bulls and goats, could never actually take away sin. In the framework of fulfilled eschatology, this scene captures the essential legal crisis that necessitated the cross and the transition to a New Covenant.

The Sovereign Rebuke and the Great Exchange (Verses 4-5)

God does not deny the filth of the garments, nor does He argue with the adversary about Joshua's guilt. Instead, God issues a sovereign rebuke based entirely on His grace: "Isn't this a burning stick plucked out of the fire?" God then orders the filthy garments to be removed and replaced with "rich clothing" and a clean turban.

This is a magnificent picture of justification by faith. The high priest is not told to go wash his own clothes; the righteousness is imputed to him purely by divine decree. When Christ inaugurated the New Covenant, He stripped away the condemned legalistic framework of the old system and clothed His people in His perfect righteousness. The adversary’s mouth is stopped not by human effort, but by divine substitution.

The Shadow of a Better Priesthood (Verses 6-8)

After Joshua is clothed, he receives conditional promises: "If you will walk in my ways..." The Old Covenant was inherently conditional and temporary. However, the angel immediately shifts the focus from Joshua’s physical ministry to the future reality. Joshua and his fellow priests are declared to be "men who are a sign." They are merely placeholders.

The substance of the sign is then revealed: "I will bring out my servant, the Branch." This is the promise of Jesus Christ. The Levitical priesthood, with its earthly temple and physical washings, was merely a shadow of the heavenly priesthood of Christ. When the Branch arrived in the first century, the sign had served its purpose. By AD 70, the physical priesthood was entirely eradicated because the true, eternal High Priest had taken His seat.

The Cornerstone of the New Temple (Verse 9)

God sets a stone before Joshua with seven eyes, declaring He will engrave it Himself. In apocalyptic literature, "seven" denotes perfection and completeness, and "eyes" denote the Spirit's omniscience. This imagery is perfectly mirrored in Revelation 5:6, where the Lamb has seven eyes, representing the sevenfold Spirit of God sent out into all the earth.

This stone is the foundation of the New Creation temple. While Joshua was building a temple of physical rocks, God was preparing to build a spiritual house upon Christ, the chief cornerstone (1 Peter 2:4-5). The engraving by God’s own hand signifies the permanence and divine origin of the New Covenant, a kingdom that cannot be shaken or destroyed by Roman armies.

Atonement in a Single Day (Verses 9b-10)

The climax of the chapter is the stunning promise: "I will remove the iniquity of that land in one day." For centuries, the high priest entered the Holy of Holies year after year on the Day of Atonement, offering sacrifices that had to be endlessly repeated. The guilt was merely rolled back, never permanently removed.

But the Branch accomplished what the Levitical system could not. At the cross, Jesus Christ removed the iniquity of the people in one single day. Hebrews 10 echoes this triumph loudly: "By one offering he has perfected forever those who are being sanctified." The resulting peace, pictured as sitting under the vine and fig tree, is not a future geopolitical millennium; it is the spiritual rest, peace, and security presently enjoyed by every believer living in the fulfilled reality of the New Covenant.

8. New Creation Reality

You are no longer standing in the divine courtroom wearing the filthy garments of your own performance or the condemnation of the law. The New Creation reality is that the adversary has no legal ground to accuse you, because the Branch has definitively removed your iniquity in one day and clothed you permanently in His rich righteousness.

9. Reflection

When the "adversary" of guilt or past failure tries to accuse you today, how does the fulfilled reality of your "rich clothing" in Christ change your response to those accusations?
