2 Peter 3

Heavens and Earth as Covenant Language (Not Planet Destruction)

2 Peter 3 is commonly read as a prediction of the physical universe melting. In this project, it is read through the Bible’s own prophetic idiom: “heavens and earth” frequently functions as covenant-order language — a world of worship, authority, identity, and access — not astronomy.

What Peter is addressing

  • Scoffers rejecting judgment because “everything continues as it always has.”
  • Covenant accountability: God intervenes in history; He has judged before; He will judge again.
  • Transition language: a passing order and the arrival of a new order “where righteousness dwells.”

Key claims in a fulfilled / covenant frame

  • “The world that then existed” (Flood) is Peter’s example that “world” language can describe an order judged, not the annihilation of matter.
  • “Day of the Lord” is prophetic-judgment language used throughout the Scriptures for covenant reckoning in history.
  • “Elements” can be understood as the basic “elements” of the old covenant order (its system-structure), not the periodic table.
  • “New heavens and new earth” is the Bible’s phrase for new covenant reality — a world reordered in righteousness.
