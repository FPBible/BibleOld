Isaiah — Appendix

Key Themes and Reading Anchors

This appendix is a map. It is not a full commentary. It highlights the themes that shape how Isaiah is used in the New Testament and how Isaiah’s “end-of-world” imagery functions covenantally.

1) Covenant Lawsuit: Judgment With Moral Clarity

Isaiah repeatedly frames judgment as a covenant lawsuit: God exposes hypocrisy, oppression, false worship, and corrupt leadership. Judgment is not random anger. It is moral truth applied to a covenant people.

2) The Holy One of Israel: God’s Character as the Centre

Isaiah’s God is not negotiated with. He is holy, faithful, and sovereign. This holiness does not eliminate mercy — it defines it. Restoration is not sentimental; it is righteous.

3) The Servant: Suffering, Healing, Vindication

The servant theme carries Isaiah’s hope forward. The New Testament repeatedly reads Jesus through Isaiah’s servant lens: rejection, suffering, bearing sin, and vindication.

4) Zion: Covenant Centre and Renewed Identity

Zion language in Isaiah often represents the covenant centre of worship and identity. Promises of Zion’s renewal describe restored covenant life — not merely territory.

5) Nations: The Expansion of Belonging

Isaiah anticipates the nations turning to the God of Israel. The New Testament presents this not as a side theme, but as the formation of one renewed people in Christ.

6) New Heavens and New Earth: Covenant Renewal Language

Isaiah’s new creation language (Isaiah 65–66) is not a science statement. It is prophetic imagery describing a renewed covenant world — new worship, new identity, new community life — in contrast to the old system marked by condemnation and death.
