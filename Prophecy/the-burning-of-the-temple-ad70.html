The Burning of the Temple

The Gates of Lebanon

Zechariah 11:1 begins with the command: "Open your doors, Lebanon, that the fire may devour your cedars." In Hebrew prophecy, "Lebanon" and its "cedars" often symbolized the Temple in Jerusalem, which was built with vast quantities of imported cedar wood. The open doors signify a loss of divine protection.

The AD 70 Fulfillment

The Jewish historian Josephus and the Talmud both record that the massive temple doors, which usually required twenty men to move, swung open by themselves just prior to the Roman destruction in AD 70. This was recognized as a fulfillment of Zechariah’s omen. The fire that devoured the "cedars" was the Roman torch, signaling that God had officially departed from His physical house because the "spiritual house" (the Church) was now built.
