The Mystery of Gentile Inclusion

"Many Nations Shall Join"

Zechariah 2:11 provides a stunning messianic promise: "Many nations shall join themselves to Yahweh in that day, and shall be my people." For a Jew under the Mosaic Law, the "nations" (Gentiles) were ritualistically unclean and separated from the covenant by the "dividing wall of hostility" (Ephesians 2:14).

The Revealed Mystery

The Apostle Paul explains that the inclusion of the Gentiles was a "mystery" hidden for ages but now revealed (Ephesians 3:3-6). This mystery is that the Gentiles are fellow heirs and partakers of the promise in Christ through the Gospel. It was not a secondary plan or a future parenthetical age; it was the intended fulfillment of the Abrahamic covenant to bless all families of the earth.

Fulfilled Equality

In the New Creation, there is no longer "Jew nor Greek" (Galatians 3:28). The "day" Zechariah spoke of arrived in the first century, particularly at the house of Cornelius and the subsequent apostolic missions. The transition of the covenants was validated when God "roused himself" from His holy habitation to welcome all peoples into a single, unified spiritual body—the Church.
